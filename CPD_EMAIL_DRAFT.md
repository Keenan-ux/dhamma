# Draft email to the Critical Pāli Dictionary / Cologne + PTS

**To:** cpd-contact@uni-koeln.de
**Cc:** info@palitextsociety.org
**Subject:** Permission request, mirroring the CPD on a non-commercial scholarly site

Dear Critical Pāli Dictionary team,

I'm writing to ask whether you would be willing to permit a small
scholarly tool I maintain at <https://dhamma.fly.dev/> to mirror the
CPD data for non-commercial use, with full attribution to the Royal
Danish Academy, the Pali Text Society, and the Cologne Data Center
for the Humanities.

The site is a comparative Pāli studies workbench: the full Tipiṭaka
with multi-translator English coverage, the major VRI/CST
commentaries and sub-commentaries, SuttaCentral parallels, and an
integrated dictionary lookup that already includes DPD, DPPN, the
PTS PED, Monier-Williams, and Edgerton's BHS. The Cologne
digitisations of MW and BHS are integrated under your sister
project's open release. The CPD would be the natural complement on
the Pāli side. You can verify by trying any term, for example
<https://dhamma.fly.dev/dict/dhamma>.

I want to be straightforward. I'm aware the CPD's online edition at
cpd.uni-koeln.de is not published as a redistributable dataset. A
scraped SQLite of the data circulates via the Digital Pāli
Dictionary's *other-dictionaries* repository on GitHub, but it
carries no licence claim and I'd rather not ingest it on that basis.
Hence this letter. I'd much prefer to have your explicit permission,
or a clear no, than to act on ambiguity.

The constraints I'd commit to:

  * Non-commercial only. The tool will never carry advertising,
    paywalls, or commercial features.
  * Full attribution displayed on every CPD entry: original editors,
    publication year, the Royal Danish Academy and PTS as
    rights-holders, and a link back to cpd.uni-koeln.de.
  * Whatever specific licence or attribution form you'd like
    applied. Please tell me and I'll honour it.

If the answer is no, that's also a clear and useful reply. I'll note
CPD as upstream-only and direct users to the Cologne search
interface from within the site.

With deep respect for the decades of work that went into the CPD,

[name withheld]
keenan@boothcheck.com
