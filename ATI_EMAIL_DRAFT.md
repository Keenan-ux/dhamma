# Draft email to Access to Insight / Barre Center for Buddhist Studies

**Subject:** Mirroring Access to Insight at dhamma.fly.dev — a heads-up and a thank you

Dear Access to Insight team,

Two reasons for writing: to thank you for the decades of work that
made Access to Insight what it is, and to let you know — straight-
forwardly — that I've downloaded the offline edition you provide for
re-use and am incorporating it into a small Buddhist studies tool at
<https://dhamma.fly.dev/>.

The site started as an idea on a Satipaṭṭhāna retreat in the Goenka
tradition: a search tool that understands meaning, not just words, so
"the chief disciple known for wisdom" returns Sāriputta even when
those exact words don't appear. It's built on the Pali canon
(SuttaCentral bilara-data, VRI/CST commentaries), four dictionaries
(DPD, DPPN, PTS PED, Monier-Williams), and a BGE-M3 vector index. No
ads, no tracking, no revenue — non-commercial throughout.

When I saw ATI is winding down I was sad — not least because my wife
reads through your pages regularly. The breadth of translators you
make accessible is something SuttaCentral's single-translator approach
doesn't match, and the curated articles and indexes are irreplaceable.

Within the CC BY-NC 4.0 terms: 1,139 ATI translations are now ingested
as additional translator options alongside Sujato, each attributed to
the translator and to "From Access to Insight (Offline Edition
2013.12.01.01)" with a link back to your page. 386 Library articles —
study guides, author essays, the Thai forest collection, Path to
Freedom, glossary — are also live. As a concrete example you can
verify, Bhikkhu Bodhi's *A Look at the Kalama Sutta* is at
<https://dhamma.fly.dev/library/ati-lib-authors-bodhi-bps-essay-09>.

I'd rather hear your perspective sooner than later. If there's any
specific way you'd like the attribution displayed, any content you'd
prefer I exclude, or any other constraint to respect, please tell me
and I'll honour it.

If it's ever useful, I'd be glad to discuss hosting a stable mirror —
this content shouldn't have to depend on any one institution's
operating budget to remain accessible.

With deep respect for the work you've done,

— [Your name]
