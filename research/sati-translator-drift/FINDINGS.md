# Sati translator-drift: memory to mindfulness at the English-translator boundary

## Abstract

The house thesis (H1) held that the English rendering of sati shifts with translator vintage: older, more philologically oriented translators (nanamoli, walshe, piyadassi) were predicted to keep a non-trivial share of memory and recollection glosses, while newer high-volume translators (sujato, thanissaro, bodhi-extract) were predicted to collapse sati toward mindfulness as a near-default. The pre-committed margin was an absolute gap of at least 5 percentage points in memory-share, direction old over new, surviving a matched-passage restriction. The corpus does not support it. Over the executed gloss pass, pooled memory-share is 3.36 percent for the old pole (5 of 149 renderings) and 4.11 percent for the new pole (38 of 925). The old-minus-new gap is about minus 0.75 percentage points: the sign is reversed and the magnitude is roughly one-seventh of the predicted threshold. The negative control did not stay flat, which under the control-must-not-move rule independently forces the null. Two falsifiers fire (memory-share under 5 percent for the whole old pole; control moves). The verdict is the pre-committed null H0. This is a statement about English rendering policy in this corpus only. It says nothing about the Pali sense of sati.

## The question and why it is a fair test

The claim is corpus-linguistic, not doctrinal. It does not ask what sati means. It asks whether the English word a translator chooses for sati tracks that translator's vintage. The test is fair because the prediction was registered in advance with a numeric threshold (a 5 percentage-point old-over-new gap in memory-share), a fixed name-to-pole assignment (old = nanamoli, walshe, piyadassi; new = sujato, thanissaro, bodhi-extract), a primary measure that is a per-translator gloss-share rather than a raw count, and a matched-passage pass designed to neutralize the obvious confound that translators do not all render the same passages. A negative control was also pre-registered: a non-sati abstract term of comparable register should not show the same old-vs-new split, otherwise any sati pattern would read as generic translator idiolect rather than term-specific drift.

Two deviations from the protocol must be stated up front. First, the planned negative control was panna (wisdom), a matched-register abstract-semantic term; the executed run substituted a bhikkhu-stem register term (monk / mendicant / kept-bhikkhu). The panna queries were never run. Second, the lexicographic baseline leg (H1c, dictionary sense-ordering) errored at the database level (no headword column) and is untested, not falsified. Neither deviation is needed to reach the verdict, because the verdict rests on the gloss-share gap and on a control that moved.

## What the canon shows

Across the executed gloss pass, mindfulness dominates the high-volume modern translators and the memory share is low everywhere. Sujato renders sati as mindfulness in 79.29 percent of cases (578 of 729) and as memory in 3.70 percent (27 of 729). Thanissaro is mindfulness 77.01 percent (144 of 187) and memory 5.35 percent (10 of 187). On the old pole, nanamoli is memory 4.07 percent (5 of 123), with mindfulness at 24.39 percent (30 of 123) masked by a large 66.67 percent OTHER bucket (82 of 123) of fragmentary or section-header renderings the deterministic matcher left unclassified. Walshe is memory 0 percent (0 of 18) and mindfulness 66.67 percent (12 of 18). Piyadassi is memory 0 percent (0 of 8) and mindfulness 100 percent (8 of 8).

Pooling by pole: old memory is 5 of 149 = 3.36 percent; new memory is 38 of 925 = 4.11 percent. The old-minus-new gap is about minus 0.75 percentage points. The predicted gap was at least plus 5 points. The observed result is the wrong sign and about one-seventh of the threshold magnitude. The whole old pole sits under 5 percent memory-share, which is exactly the condition falsifier 1 names.

The matched and forced-context passes do not rescue the thesis. In the source-stratum split, nanamoli's only memory hits (4) sit in the commentary stratum (5comm); within the shared early-canon stratum (1early) the old translators are mindfulness or OTHER with near-zero memory, so no within-passage old-over-new drift appears. In the recollection-context pass, where the Pali is in an explicit anussati / recollection compound and a memory gloss is most licensed, modern sujato carries recollection-English MORE than the old translators (128 of 128 versus nanamoli 33 of 80), again the opposite of the predicted direction.

A contradicting data point sits outside the pre-committed poles: brahmali, a modern translator, shows the highest memory-share of all (27.59 percent, 16 of 58). brahmali was not assigned to either vintage pole in advance and is reported as a contradicting observation, not folded silently into a pole. Its presence further undercuts any clean old-over-new story.

## What the commentary does and the falsifiable core

Native Pali memory-lexeme density is reported as a descriptive supplement only and is barred from convergent evidence, because memory words are memory words and any stratum signal there is true by construction. The descriptive figures are 4.05 hits per Mchar in the early canon (1early mula), 27.30 in the commentary (attha), and 22.57 in the sub-commentary (tika). These describe where native memory vocabulary lives in the Pali; they do not bear on the English-rendering question and are not counted as support.

The falsifiable core of H1 was the per-translator gloss-share gap: old pole memory-share at least 5 points above new pole, surviving the matched-passage restriction, with a non-moving control. The gap is sign-reversed and far below threshold; the matched and forced-context passes do not produce the predicted direction; the control moved. Two of the five pre-registered falsifiers fire directly.

## The negative-control result

The control did not stay flat, and a moving control forces the null regardless of the signal.

The executed control is the bhikkhu-stem register term. Translators diverge sharply on it. Sujato renders bhikkhu as mendicant 1045 times against monk 144; thanissaro uses monk 260 times and mendicant 0; brahmali monk 253 and mendicant 0; nanamoli keeps bhikkhu untranslated 43 times with monk only twice. Per-translator rendering entropy on the control confirms the divergence is real and, critically, runs in the SAME direction as the supposed sati signal: the old translators carry the highest control entropy (olendzki 1.0114, nanamoli 0.6941, walshe 0.6002 nats) while the modern high-volume translators are low (sujato 0.2245, thanissaro 0.1115 nats). A plain non-sati term already shows an old-vs-new split as large as or larger than anything seen for sati. Under the program rule that a finding stands only if its negative control does not move with it, and under the protocol's own falsifier 3, the moving control forces the null.

Two limits on the control must travel with this result. The control was a substitution: the planned panna control was never run, and the bhikkhu proxy is a weaker, register-matched stand-in rather than the like-for-like abstract-semantic comparison. The control statistic also differs from the signal statistic: the sati metric is a 4-way gloss-share, while the executed control is a rendering-token entropy. The control therefore does two things and not a third. It demonstrates that an old-vs-new split exists on a non-sati term, and it is sufficient to force the null. It does NOT positively prove that the (absent) sati pattern shares the same idiolect mechanism. The bhikkhu split is driven by transliteration-retention and monk-versus-mendicant lexical habit, which is a coverage and word-choice artifact, not a demonstration that sati gloss-sense divergence runs on that same engine.

## What it means and what it does not

It means that in this corpus there is no measurable translator-vintage drift of sati from memory toward mindfulness. Mindfulness is the dominant rendering across both poles, the old pole does not carry a higher memory-share than the new pole (it carries a slightly lower one), and a register-matched control already moves at least as much as sati. The memory-to-mindfulness narrative, as a diachronic translation shift detectable here, is not supported. This is the pre-committed H0.

It does not mean memory rendering is absent from older translators, and it does not adjudicate the well-known scholarly debate about the Pali sense of sati. That debate concerns what the Pali word means; this is a corpus null about English rendering policy and bears on it not at all. The prior literature must not leak in as if this null settled it.

Several limits bound the strength of the null. The old pole rests on tiny denominators (149 renderings total; piyadassi n=8, walshe n=18; the only old-pole memory hits are 5 from nanamoli, sitting in the commentary stratum), so the full-corpus pass is coverage-confounded and is reported as supporting, not decisive. The gloss shares are unvalidated deterministic-regex auto-labels: the human Cohen kappa coding pass was not executed to a coefficient at the time of this write-up, and nanamoli's 66.67 percent OTHER bucket (82 of 123) is unclassified mass that could hide memory or mindfulness glosses the lexicon missed. Any share figure is provisional until auto-vs-human agreement clears the 0.60 floor (falsifier 4). The result reads as no drift detected at low power with an unvalidated labeler, not drift proven absent. The robustness of the null to the unvalidated labeler comes only from the gap being sign-reversed and roughly one-seventh of threshold, far outside any plausible coding wobble. The blind coding that was run shows high agreement where codings coincide, and is reported as consistent with the null call only to that extent; it does not by itself certify the share figures. H1c (the lexicographic baseline) is withdrawn as untested, not reported as support in either direction. Vintage is a fixed name-to-pole proxy with no per-rendering publication year, and stratum is a work_slug lookup, not independent chronology, so a layer contrast is true by construction and is never cited as independent evidence.

## Auditability

Every count re-derives from _protocol.json (the frozen lexica, queries, falsifiers, and pole assignment) and _raw.json (the executed query results). Old pooled memory 5 of 149 = 3.36 percent and new pooled 38 of 925 = 4.11 percent come from query 04 (04_gloss_share_by_translator_DRIFT_PRIMARY): nanamoli memory 5 of 123, walshe 0 of 18, piyadassi 0 of 8, sujato 27 of 729, thanissaro 10 of 187, bodhi 1 of 9. The gap of about minus 0.75 points is 3.36 minus 4.11. Sujato mindfulness 578 of 729 = 79.29 percent and thanissaro 144 of 187 = 77.01 percent are query 04. The stratum split (nanamoli memory in 5comm only; 1early near-zero) is query 05. The recollection-context counts (sujato 128 of 128, nanamoli 33 of 80) are query 06. The control monk-vs-mendicant counts (sujato 1045 mendicant / 144 monk; thanissaro 260 monk / 0 mendicant; brahmali 253 monk; nanamoli 43 bhikkhu-kept / 2 monk) are query 08; the control entropy (olendzki 1.0114, nanamoli 0.6941, walshe 0.6002, sujato 0.2245, thanissaro 0.1115 nats) is query 09. The descriptive native-memory density (4.05, 27.30, 22.57 per Mchar) is query 02. The lexicographic baseline (query 10) returned a database ERROR (no headword column) and is recorded as untested. The brahmali contradicting figure (16 of 58 = 27.59 percent) is query 04. The substituted control and the planned-but-unrun panna control are noted as protocol deviations.

## Scope

Translator-boundary study, not a Pali-semantics study; a null says nothing about the sense of sati. Vintage is a fixed name-to-pole assignment, a coarse proxy for diachrony with no per-rendering year. Coverage is uneven and the full-corpus pass is passage-mix confounded. Gloss shares are unvalidated deterministic auto-labels pending a human kappa pass. The negative control was a register-matched substitution, not the planned abstract-term control. The lexicographic leg is untested. Native memory density is descriptive supplement only and barred from convergent evidence.
