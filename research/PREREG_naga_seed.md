## Central question

What does the Pāli canon hold a **nāga** *to be* — ontologically and
soteriologically — and how do the Aṭṭhakathā and Ṭīkā systematize, harden, or
soften that picture?

## Three analytical segments (kept distinct in the dataset, not blended)

- **§A — Granular ontology (the spine).** A systematic enumeration of what a nāga
  *is*: the four *nāgayoni* (aṇḍaja / jalābuja / saṃsedaja / opapātika, SN 29),
  realm and habitat, lifespan, powers (shapeshifting, weather, venom, assuming
  human form), classification under *tiracchāna-yoni*, and the karma that causes
  rebirth as one. The "just what are they" layer, at the finest granularity the
  corpus supports.
- **§B — The soteriological paradox.** Every text bearing on the ceiling: a nāga
  keeps uposatha, hears Dhamma, takes human form, yet cannot attain magga-phala.
  Includes the *manussosi* ("are you human?") ordination rule and its
  nāga-infiltration origin story (Vin Mahāvagga).
- **§C — Canon → commentary divergence.** For each finding in §A/§B, what the
  commentary adds, regiments, or quietly shifts versus the sutta layer.

## Enumeration boundary — inclusive (all confirmed in scoping)

- Nāgasaṃyutta (SN 29) — the four nāgayoni + rebirth/uposatha mechanics
- Vinaya ordination rule + its nāga-infiltration origin (Vin Mahāvagga)
- Nāga-king Jātakas — Bhūridatta, Campeyya, Saṅkhapāla
- Mucalinda (Ud 2.1) and parallels — the benevolent sheltering nāga
- Garuḷa / supaṇṇa predation material — the nāga's place in the cosmic food chain
- Scattered AN / MN / DN canonical hits + their commentarial paragraph-rows

## Critical method — sense disambiguation up front (MUST be in the frozen prereg)

A raw `nāga` exact search returns **200+ rows** (capped), blended across four
referents:

1. **serpent-being** (the study's target),
2. **elephant** (the noble tusker, simile material),
3. **Buddha/arahant epithet** (the folk etymology *na + āgu*, "does no wrong"),
4. **tree** (Mesua ferrea / ironwood).

The study's first coded step is classifying every hit by referent and carrying
forward **only the serpent-being sense**, reporting the exclusion counts. The
disambiguation is itself a reportable finding (nāga is a canonical heteronym),
not a footnote. This gate is what keeps the enumeration honest.

## Enumeration unit

Passage-row (mūla + per-paragraph commentary). Every citation must resolve to a
live corpus row. Canon-vs-commentary split tracked per row.
