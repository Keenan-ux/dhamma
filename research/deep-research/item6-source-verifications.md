## 1. Brahmali on cetana=kamma — CONFIRMED (high, exact-primary)
Bhikkhu Brahmali affirms the equation in his own voice:
> "I agree with Gabriel that the main meaning of saṅkhāra in the EBTs is 'intentional activity', and as such
> it is synonymous with kamma."
Source: Brahmali, SuttaCentral Discourse, thread "Explaining sankhāra='choices'", **post #22, 18 Oct 2018**
(JSON ground-truth confirms username=Brahmali; the post sits on thread page 2, which is why a naive
first-page fetch missed it). He grounds it in SN 22.56/57 (saṅkhāra = cetanā/sañcetanā) plus the standard
cetanā-defines-kamma move (AN 6.63).
**Nuance to preserve if ever cited:** "synonymous with kamma" is context-restricted to the khandha/DO sense
(he insists *sabbe saṅkhārā dukkhā* = all conditioned phenomena, a different sense), and the rendering
**"choices" is Sujato's, not Brahmali's** — Brahmali does not adopt the single-word gloss. So "choices=kamma"
is Sujato's translation strategy resting on a cetanā=kamma equation Brahmali independently affirms.

## 2. Hamilton "most important / most difficult khandha" — REFUTED (high)
No accessible source attributes a "most important" or "most difficult" khandha superlative to Sue Hamilton.
Her actual superlative (Identity and Experience, 1996, **p.71**, secondary-attributed via Piya Tan SD 17.6):
> "The saṅkhārakkhandha is unique among the khandhas in that it need not, and indeed ultimately should not,
> be 'activated' in the functioning of a human being."
The nearby superlatives belong to OTHERS: "most difficult [early Buddhist term]" is **Piya Tan** (about
saṅkhāra's polysemy, citing Boisvert); "most important factor in this aggregate" is **Bhikkhu Bodhi**
(S:B 1065 n84, about *volition within* saṅkhāra). The saṅkhāra chapter is pp.66-81 (ch.4).
**FOLD INTO ITEM 4 (Hamilton census):** the headline "the khandha×āyatana×dhātu correlations are not made
explicit in the Nikāyas at all, but only in the Abhidhamma Piṭaka, which classifies the sense bases under
rūpa" is **Bhikkhu Bodhi commenting on SN 35.1** (Connected Discourses), NOT Hamilton. Hamilton's own related
move is the OPPOSITE: she *challenges* placing the five sense faculties under rūpakkhandha (the faculties
partake of both material and mental characteristics). So the item-4 cross-classification census should
attribute the "only in the Abhidhamma" claim to **Bodhi**, and should not expect a Hamilton superlative.
Source-access limit: Hamilton's own text is not cleanly openable (archive.org OCR mis-detected the font as
Devanagari; body prose garbled), so p.71 is secondary-attributed via Piya Tan (a meticulous page-citer).

## 3. Bucknell 1993 on vitakka-vicara — CONFIRMED (high, exact-primary)
Roderick S. Bucknell, "Reinterpreting the Jhānas," JIABS 16.2 (1993): 375-409. Full machine-readable PDF
recovered (the "image-only scan" worry was wrong; pdftotext extracted 82,578 chars with intact page headers).
Bucknell's thesis in his OWN voice (**p.397**):
> "These various considerations support the identification of vitakka-vicara with the normal flow of thought;
> the suppression of vitakka-vicara in the transition from jhana 1 to jhana 2 is the meditative achievement
> of bringing the flow of thought to a standstill."
**Citation nuance:** the most-quoted sentence (p.376, "the normal process of discursive thought, the familiar
but usually unnoticed stream of mental imagery and verbalization") is Bucknell *summarizing Griffiths and
Stuart-Fox*, which he then adopts. For an unambiguous own-words cite, **prefer p.397 over p.376**. He also
prefers "concentration" over "absorption" for jhāna because thinking is present in the first jhāna, and reads
the later "initial application" gloss of vitakka as an ad hoc adjustment once ekaggatā was attributed to
jhāna 1.

## 4. Shankman 2008 on sutta-vs-Visuddhimagga jhana — MIXED (high)
The wording is GENUINE Shankman (confirmed against his own Sati Center teaching handout reproducing the
book's Conclusion):
> "There are two distinct jhanas in the Pali literature, each one true and correct within its own system:
> 'sutta jhana' and 'Visuddhimagga jhana'."
Source: Richard Shankman, The Experience of Samadhi (Shambhala 2008), **Conclusion of Part One**.
**Two corrections for any future citation:**
- The page locator **"p.104" is wrong** (it traces to an unpaginated Goodreads page). Same-edition footnotes
  place the comparative material at pp.80/117/137-138, so the Conclusion is **~pp.139-144**. Book page
  unverified (could not OCR the scanned PDF locally); cite "Conclusion" rather than a wrong page.
- **Shankman does NOT say the suttas reject absorption.** He holds samadhi is "indispensable for attainment
  of arahantship"; the live dispute is the *type* of jhana (mindful, body-aware, insight-permeable vs. fixed
  one-pointed absorption), not whether deep concentration is needed. Any future IG/vitakka sentence must not
  read him as denying a concentration requirement.
