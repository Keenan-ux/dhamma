# The Theravada self-label across canon and commentary: a density and frame profile

Verdict: mixed. The density prediction (H1, density leg) holds after a mandatory ingest-artifact correction. The frame prediction (H1, frame leg) is not adjudicable from structure in this corpus and awaits a blind coding pass. The pre-committed null H0(a) is rejected; H0(b) remains open.

## Abstract

This study profiles five self-designation stems for the Theravada tradition (theravada, theriya, vibhajjavada, tambapanniya, mahaviharavasin) by per-character density across three text layers (canon, atthakatha, tika) and by the syntactic company the labels keep. The house thesis (H1) is that the self-label is essentially absent from the mula canon as a school name, rises to a commentarial ceiling, and where it does appear reads predominantly as an elder-lineage or teachers-opinion equation (vada as a near-synonym of nikaya, the Gethin frame) rather than as a sectarian school-name set against named rivals. The raw census appears to falsify the density prediction: the canon shows 14.804 self-label-bearing passages per Mchar, commentarial-grade. Inspection shows 369 of the 381 canon hits are a single ingest artifact, the modern edition collection title "Theravada Vinaya Bhikkhunivibhanga ..." sitting at character 0 of every pli-vinaya passage, not a body token. After removing that header string the canon density falls to 0.466 per Mchar against an atthakatha ceiling of 5.539 and a tika ceiling of 5.290, an eleven to twelve fold canon-to-commentary rise, with a single visible elder-lineage trace surviving in canon (thi-ap28, "theriyo ca mahiddhika"). The negative control (thera alone) is high and roughly flat across layers and does not reproduce the self-label jump, so the rise is not a generic elder-talk register or a length artifact. The density prediction therefore holds and H0(a) is rejected. The frame leg cannot be settled from structure: the protocol's nominated contrastive locus, the Kathavatthu-atthakatha, is entirely absent from this corpus (no kv work_slug exists), and the rival co-occurrence count is contaminated by both the header artifact and a darkness-word false positive. The frame leg rests on a blind coding pass that has not yet completed.

## The question and why it is a fair test

The label "Theravada" is read two ways. One reading treats it as the name of a bounded sect, contrastively coordinated with rival schools (Mahimsasaka, Sabbatthivada, Andhaka, and so on). The other, associated with Gethin, treats vada as standing in for nikaya or acariyavada, so that "theravada" heads an equation meaning the elders' position or the lineage of the elders, an internal doctrinal sense rather than a sectarian self versus other. The corpus cannot decide which reading is doctrinally correct. It can measure two things that bear on the linguistic question: how the per-character density of the self-label moves from canon to commentary, and what kinds of terms the label co-occurs with.

The test is fair because it carries its own falsifiers and controls. If the canon already carried the self-label at commentarial density, the "canon supple, commentary systematizes" thesis would be falsified outright. If a generic elder-register term (thera alone, without the -vada or theriy- stem) rose canon-to-commentary by the same factor as the self-label, the apparent crystallization would be an artifact of register or text length, and the pre-committed null H0(a) would win. A positive control for the sectarian frame (real rival school names) tests whether the label actually keeps contrastive company. Ingest-defect guards (dental-n tambapanniy, short-a mahavihar) test whether diacritic-correct regexes are silently missing normalized text.

Density is computed per character, never as raw row counts: density equals the count of is_primary passages whose text matches the diacritic-correct regex, divided by the Mchar of that layer. The layer assignment is a work_slug lookup. A canon-to-commentary gradient is therefore a usage-ceiling pattern in the commentarial register, not dated evidence of when the term arose.

## What the canon shows

The raw canon census reads 381 self-label-bearing passages over 25.737131 Mchar, a density of 14.804 per Mchar. Taken at face value this matches the commentarial layers and would falsify the density prediction (canon predicted near-zero). It does not survive inspection. 369 of the 381 canon hits are a document-header ingest artifact: each is a pli-vinaya bhikkhuni or parivara passage whose snippet begins at character 0 with the modern edition collection title "Theravada Vinaya Bhikkhunivibhanga ..." (the SuttaCentral-style header), not a body token. The artifact is canon-specific: no atthakatha, tika, or other-layer passage carries the self-label as a title-position token. After stripping the header string the genuine canon count falls to 12, a density of 0.466 per Mchar. The correction is what distinguishes the readings: on the uncorrected numbers H1 is falsified, on the corrected numbers it is supported, so the correction is mandatory rather than optional, and the artifact is disclosed in full.

Of the 12 survivors, only one (thi-ap28, "theriyo ca mahiddhika") carries a visible self-label token inside its 600-character snippet, and that one is the elder-lineage register (theriya, "of the elders"). The other 11 are oversized CST coarse-vagga passages (for example pli-tv-pvr1.1 at 89,363 characters, mn85 at 42,990) whose matched stem sits deep in long bodies beyond the snippet window and is not inspectable from this data. The claim that all 12 are the elder or teachers-statement reading, and the claim that none sits in a school-contrast frame, are therefore carried by a single visible row and are provisional pending the blind coding pass for the other 11.

The corrected canon floor is a per-passage incidence rate, not a per-token rate. Density counts passages that match the regex one or more times, so a single occurrence inside an 89,363-character vagga weighs identically to a single 600-character passage. The 0.466 per Mchar floor is edition-granularity-sensitive and is reported as a passage-incidence rate.

## What the commentary does, and the falsifiable core

Density rises sharply into the commentary. The atthakatha layer reads 170 hits over 30.691214 Mchar (5.539 per Mchar); the tika layer reads 150 over 28.357746 Mchar (5.290 per Mchar); the other layer reads 67 over 9.880513 Mchar (6.781 per Mchar). Against the corrected canon floor of 0.466 this is a rise of about 11.9 fold to atthakatha and 11.4 fold to tika. The tika adds no ceiling beyond the atthakatha (5.290 versus 5.539, essentially equal). The corrected gradient matches the predicted shape: canon near-zero with an elder-lineage trace surviving, commentary carrying essentially the whole cluster.

The frame question is whether the commentary systematizes a lineage-of-elders identity (the equation frame) or a crystallized sect set against rivals (the contrast frame). Structure gives a partial answer. The equation co-occurrence pass (self-label with vada, nikaya, acariyavada, kula heads) totals 64 across 17 work_slugs, led by pli-vin-tika (21). The rival-contrast co-occurrence pass (self-label with named rival schools) totals 37 across 12 work_slugs. The equation frame leads structurally, but not overwhelmingly, and two problems block any verdict from structure alone.

First, the protocol's nominated contrastive locus is absent. H1 predicts the rival-contrast frame concentrates in the Kathavatthu-atthakatha. No kv work_slug exists anywhere in this corpus, mula or attha. The Kathavatthu-atthakatha reproduction pass (query 07) returned zero rows. The "concentrated in Kv-a" sub-prediction is therefore not-evaluable here, neither confirmed nor refuted, and the related falsifier cannot be tested.

Second, the rival-contrast total of 37 is contaminated on both legs. The single largest rival co-occurrence bucket is again pli-vinaya (14), whose self-label leg is the header artifact. The rival leg of those same pli-vinaya rows matches only through the darkness vocabulary "Rattandhakaravagga" and "andhakare," not the Andhaka school: the rival-name control stem andhak (no word boundary) matches andhakara (darkness) and rattandhakara (a vagga title), inflating both the query-04 contrast total and the query-03 canon rival-name density (6.178 per Mchar). The genuine rival-contrast count is lower than 37 and no contrast magnitude should be cited until the rival regex is re-run boundary-tight to exclude andhakara.

The frame leg therefore cannot be adjudicated from structure. It rests on the blind double-coding pass over the 768 query-06 rows. Three blind coders have coded those rows; inter-annotator kappa is computed deterministically downstream. Where the coders agree, agreement is high, and the coding supports the load-distinguishing call only to the extent the coders coincide. Per the pre-committed thresholds the frame proportions are reported only if kappa reaches 0.67 for the relevant codebook; the 369 header-artifact rows screen out first and are excluded from all proportions. The frame leg's published-thesis character (this restates Gethin's vada-equals-nikaya thesis) means the corpus contributes a density quantification, not a new conclusion.

## The negative-control result

The controls behaved, and crucially none of them tracked the self-label upward, so the density finding stands by the program rule.

The generic elder-register control, thera alone, is high and roughly flat across layers: canon 38.310, atthakatha 97.813, tika 35.193, other 19.129 per Mchar. It does not reproduce the self-label's near-zero-to-ceiling jump. The self-label rises about 12 fold from a 0.466 canon floor; thera alone stays within a roughly two to three fold band and is actually lower in tika and other than at its atthakatha peak. The crystallization signal is therefore not a generic elder-talk register or a length artifact, and the pre-committed null H0(a) is rejected.

The rival-nikaya-name control is undramatic: canon 6.178, atthakatha 18.083, tika 12.378, other 6.477 per Mchar, with no canonical zero and no commentarial spike. It does not move with the self-label signal (and, as noted, its canon value is itself inflated by the andhakara false positive). The ingest-defect guards (dental-n tambapanniy, short-a mahavihar) returned 0 in every layer, confirming the diacritic-correct regexes are not silently dropping normalized text.

The one moving element inside the self-label measure is the document-header contamination itself: the 369 pli-vinaya passages carrying the editorial title at character 0. That is a header artifact, not a control failure, and once removed the canon value drops from 14.804 to 0.466 per Mchar.

## What it means and what it does not

The density prediction holds after correction: the Theravada self-label is essentially absent from the mula canon as a school label (0.466 per Mchar, a single visible elder-lineage trace), rises about 11 to 14 fold into the commentary, and the tika adds no ceiling beyond the atthakatha. The negative control did not move with it, so this is a register-density pattern, not an artifact. The pre-committed null H0(a) is rejected.

The frame prediction is not confirmed. Its predicted locus is absent from the corpus, its structural rival count is contaminated, and the canon sense reading for 11 of the 12 survivors is unverified from the snippets available. The frame leg awaits the blind coding pass, so H0(b) remains open. The overall verdict is mixed.

This is a corpus-linguistic result about labels: density, register, and structure. It is not a doctrinal verdict. It does not date the crystallization of "Theravada" as a sectarian identity (an out-of-corpus question for epigraphy and later usage), and it does not settle whether the Mahavihara was a self-conscious school. The layer gradient is a frame profile of the labels in the commentarial register, never a verdict on sectarian self-consciousness and never independent chronology, because the layer is a work_slug lookup.

## Auditability

Every count re-derives from _protocol.json and _raw.json. The canon Mchar denominator (25.737131) is the sum of the four canon strata in query 00 (13.095668 + 3.851892 + 7.576799 + 1.212772). The per-layer census counts (canon 381, atthakatha 170, tika 150, other 67, total 768) re-derive by applying the query-02 layer rule to the 768 query-06 rows. The 369 header-artifact count is the number of canon query-06 rows whose snippet begins with the collection title "Theravada Vinaya ..."; the 12 survivors and the single visible elder-lineage trace (thi-ap28) follow directly. Densities are the stated counts over the query-00 Mchar denominators. Co-occurrence totals (rival 37, equation 64) are the row sums of queries 04 and 05. The absence of any kv work_slug and the empty query-07 result are read directly from the data. Three queries erred and are reported as such: queries 01 and 02 (the primary density passes) timed out, so density was reconstructed from the query-06 census against query-00 denominators; query 09 (the thera-alone foil calibration set) failed on an "id % 97" integer-cast error, so no foil rows were generated this round. One committed denominator drifted: the protocol's atthakatha constant 29.488533 against the live query-00 value 30.691214 (a 4.1 percent rise). The live value was used, which is correct, and the drift is noted here rather than absorbed silently.

## Scope and limits

This DB cannot date the modern sectarian sense; that is an out-of-corpus question. The layer is a work_slug lookup, not chronology. No foreign-language text (Sanskrit, Chinese parallels) is in scope. The result settles no doctrinal question about the Mahavihara. CST coarse-vagga versus SC fine-sutta granularity makes distinct-work counts edition-sensitive; per-Mchar density is the edition-robust headline, and the corrected canon floor is a per-passage incidence rate. The design is a sparse census with no inferential statistics. The corrected canon floor of 12 is sense-verified for only 1 of 12 rows. The query-04 pli-vinaya bucket cannot be confirmed as a pure artifact because query 04 returns no snippets, though the census shows its rival leg is the darkness word and its self-label leg is the header. The Kathavatthu is entirely absent, so the contrast-locus sub-prediction is not-evaluable.
