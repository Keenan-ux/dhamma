## The reframe — what this study is and is NOT

This is **not** concordism. It does not argue the Sineru cosmology is true,
predicts modern physics, or secretly encodes astronomy. (That move flatters
whatever science is current and has a poor track record.) Instead it asks a
philological, redaction-critical, and *measurable* question about the tradition's
own literalism.

The cleanest empirical question about the cosmos is not "is the map true" but
**"how hard was each generation of the tradition trying to make it a map at
all."** That is answerable from the corpus, citably, by layer.

## Central question

Across the textual strata (mūla sutta → Abhidhamma → Aṭṭhakathā → Ṭīkā), **where
and when does the cosmos acquire quantitative, metric, surveyable specificity —
and is spatial literalization a late, commentarial accretion onto a canon whose
cosmological register is qualitative, relational, and observer-relative?**

## Segment §A — the measurement axis (the spine)

Every cosmological passage is **double-coded**:

- **Layer:** sutta / Abhidhamma / Aṭṭhakathā / Ṭīkā (+ extra-canonical Anya).
- **Register:** one of
  - **Schematic-qualitative** — names/relations without metric detail
    (Sineru is "the highest", continents "to the north", etc.),
  - **Quantified-metric** — explicit dimensions, counts, fixed durations
    (yojana heights, lifespans in years, plane counts, kappa lengths),
  - **Psychologized-relativized** — the cosmos read as experience / indexed to
    states of mind (jhāna-indexing of the rūpa planes; the Rohitassa
    "fathom-long body" loka).

**Headline finding = the Layer × Register cross-tab.** The directional hypothesis
(below) is that Quantified-metric concentrates in the late layers.

## Segment §B — objects enumerated (the breadth)

1. **Mount Sineru + the seven ranges and seas** — are heights/depths/distances
   ever given, and in which layer?
2. **The four continents** — sizes, shapes, fixed lifespans (ties to the
   Uttarakuru study; reuse its rows where they overlap).
3. **The world-system & its multiplicity** — the *cakkavāḷa* construct AND the
   canonical multiplicity vocabulary (thousand → million → billion *lokadhātu*,
   AN 3.80). Are the numbers stable or do they inflate across layers?
4. **Deep time (kappa)** — the four *asaṅkheyya* phases (saṃvaṭṭa → vivaṭṭa), the
   destruction-by-fire/water/wind tiers, the length-similes (mountain, mustard
   seed), and *anamatagga* (no first beginning, SN 15). Quantified vs apophatic.
5. **The vertical planes — the CONTROL.** Where the texts *themselves*
   psychologize the cosmos (rūpa planes indexed to jhāna; arūpa to the formless
   attainments; the Rohitassa relativization). Establishes that the vertical axis
   is mind, not distance — and that the psychologized reading is canonical, not a
   modern imposition.

## Directional hypothesis (pre-commit to reporting the null)

**H1:** Quantified-metric *spatial* specificity concentrates in Aṭṭhakathā/Ṭīkā;
the mūla layer's cosmological register is predominantly Schematic-qualitative or
Psychologized-relativized. What canonical quantification exists is **temporal**
(kappa / asaṅkheyya), not spatial.

**H0 (must be reported if it wins):** the suttas already quantify the cosmos
metrically at rates comparable to the commentary — i.e. literalization is *not* a
late move.

The frozen prereg must pre-commit to publishing the cross-tab whatever it shows.

## Critical method — disambiguation & scoping gates (MUST be in the frozen prereg)

1. **`yojana` is used for all distances**, most of them mundane (road/travel/
   geography). Enumeration must keep only *yojana applied to cosmological objects*
   (Sineru, continents, seas, plane-distances) and exclude ordinary itineraries.
   This filter is itself reportable.
2. **The multiplicity concept ≠ the word `cakkavāḷa`.** The suttas carry the
   plurality-of-worlds idea under *sahassī / cūḷanikā / majjhimikā / mahā-sahassī
   lokadhātu* (AN 3.80), not the term *cakkavāḷa*. Search the **concept**, not
   just the term, or the canonical layer will look empty by construction.
3. **Variant spellings** — *cakkavāḷa / cakkavāla*; *yojana* compounds.
4. **Relevance sample ≠ census.** Scoping used the search API's top-N relevance
   ranking; the study does a **full enumeration with explicit layer tags** and
   reports the proper cross-tab, not a ranked sample.

## Enumeration unit

Passage-row (mūla + per-paragraph commentary), double-coded (Layer × Register),
tagged by cosmological object. Every citation resolves to a live corpus row.

## Anchor texts (seed list, not exhaustive)

- **AN 3.80** (Abhibhū) — the thousand → million → billion *lokadhātu* scaling
- **SN 15** (Anamatagga) — *anamatagga* + the kappa length-similes
- **SN 2.26 / AN 4.45** (Rohitassa) — the observer-relative loka (the §B5 control)
- **DN 27** (Aggañña) — re-evolution / descent from Ābhassara
- **DN 32** (Āṭānāṭiya) — the northern quarter / continent frame
- **Visuddhimagga** cosmology (pli-vism + pli-vism-tika)
- **Vinaya Ṭīkā** — the yojana-dense layer flagged in scoping

## Scoping evidence (motivating numbers — exact-search by work, 2026-06-19)

- **`yojana`** — total 3,976; top-200 sample dominated by `pli-vin-tika` (145),
  plus `kn-attha`, `abh-tika`, `dn-tika`, `mn-tika`; **≈0 mūla in the sample.**
  → metric/dimensional language concentrates in ṭīkā/aṭṭhakathā.
- **`cakkavāḷa`** — total 34; **every hit commentary / ṭīkā / extra-canonical,
  zero mūla.** → the named world-system construct is post-canonical vocabulary.
- **`asaṅkheyya`** — total 10, with canonical footing (`pli-kn`, `pli-mn`,
  `pli-bv`, `pli-ap`). → canonical quantification is **temporal, not spatial.**

(These are ranked-sample counts, not a census — they motivate H1; the study tests
it by full layer-tagged enumeration. See gate #4.)
