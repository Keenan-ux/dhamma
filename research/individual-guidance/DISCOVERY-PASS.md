# Individual-guidance — wide-recall discovery pass (2026-06-20)

A discovery pass run *after* the chronology refactor, to test the methodology critique:
that the study searched essentially one word-family (`carita`) and so could only find the
pattern it already hypothesised. Method: 6 blind search-angle lenses → centralized live-corpus
recall over 80 pooled stems → 4 pattern lenses → adversarial sense-audit of every candidate
(raw ILIKE counts prove a *string* is present, not that a person-individuation pattern is real)
→ synthesis. 17 agents. Every count below was re-checked against live `dhamma-pg` rows; the
"over-collected" ledger lists what the audit pruned, so we never re-import the bad numbers.

---

## 1. Verdict on the question — DEMOTED, not invalidated

The original question ("where does the six-temperament `carita` scheme sit, and how firmly does
the tradition vouch for it?") **survives intact and every load-bearing answer re-verified**:
the temperament-sense `carita` compound is a hard categorical **0** in the four Nikāyas and the
seven Abhidhamma books; it enters at the para-canonical Khuddaka (Cūḷaniddesa `cnd19`), hardens
in the Visuddhimagga, is physiologized in the Ṭīkā, and the Vism disowns its own diagnostic
method (`na sārato paccetabbaṃ`, `kevalaṃ ācariyamatānusārena vuttaṃ`; `cst-e0101n.mul-36_p030`,
echoed in `cst-e0103n.att-37_p028`).

What moves is its **status**. The wide field shows `carita`'s late-pooling is the late corner of
a populous person-individuation field the `carita` word could not see. The reframed master axis:

> **FUNCTION-vs-ESSENCE × textual stratum.** The canon individuates persons *early* by what they
> presently **do / are afflicted by / lean toward / are capable of** (verb-of-leaning,
> present-participle, momentary). The commentary **freezes** this into fixed noun-types
> (compound-as-standing-type). `carita` is one local instance of a corpus-wide *late
> nominalization* of an early process-sense.

The chronology question does not reverse — it **splits**: function/present-state/route vocabulary
is early; fixed-noun-type vocabulary is late.

## 4. General-importance finds (the "counter-clockwise crowds" lane — orthogonal to person-matching)

- **G1 — corpus-wide ESSENTIALIZATION drift.** Genuine philosophical `sabhāva` ("own-being") is
  effectively **0** in canon-mūla (the 9 raw hits are `purisabhāva` / `ekaṃsa-bhāvita` false
  positives) and explodes to attha 1573 / vism 513 / tika 4018 — a commentary-born substantialist
  ontology. Person-essentialization (the `carita` freeze) is **one local instance** of a general
  move from "what a dhamma *does*" to "what a dhamma *is* by own-nature." A measurable datum in the
  standing scholarly **"sabhāva controversy"** about when Theravāda acquired a substantialist
  ontology. Field: ontology / philology. Confidence: high.
- **G2 — an explicit early canonical HOMOPHILY LAW with a stated mechanism.** SN 14.14
  (`sn14.14` Hīnādhimuttikasutta), 14.22, 14.25: *Dhātuso, bhikkhave, sattā saṃsandanti samenti* —
  beings flow together and combine **by element/disposition**: low-disposition with low, virtuous
  with virtuous, killers with killers, the faithless with the faithless. A Buddha-voiced
  **assortative-association / homophily principle** with a named causal mechanism, asserted as a
  *continuum before any finite person-type catalogue*. Nothing to do with practice-matching — a
  canonical sociology of how beings cluster. Welds the `nānādhātu` + `adhimutti` families.
  Field: social dynamics / canonical sociology. Confidence: high.
- **G3 — the diacritic/substring HOMOGRAPH TRAP** (corpus-linguistics caution). A naive ASCII
  census miscounts in **both** directions: over-recall (`%carita%` returns 224 canon rows, all
  `sucarita`/`duccarita`/`caritvā` conduct, 0 temperament; same for `asay`→"lair", `neyy`→gerundive,
  `vineyy`→"having removed") and under-recall (blind to the entire long-ā seven-puggala roster).
  This is *why* the carita-only study missed an early systematized typology hiding under diacritics.
- **G4 — DESCRIBE-EARLY / NAME-LATE** signature of scholastic terminology. The *practice* is
  canonical but the *technical noun* naming it is commentarial: `anupubbim kathaṃ` (graduated talk)
  ~27 canon but the bound compound `anupubbikathā` is 3 canon / 119–151 attha; same for
  `asayānusaya`, `veneyya`, the carita-grid, the vehicle-split. The nominalization itself is the
  late event — the function-vs-essence axis seen from the pedagogy side.

## 5. Adversarial ledger — DO NOT re-import these (pruned by the sense-audit)

- **`carita` 224 canon-mūla = homograph noise** (all `su-`/`duc-carita` conduct + `caritvā`); the
  headline rests on the sense-disambiguated **compound** only (0 canon).
- **`anusaya` 119 and `cetopariya` 121 are mostly FIXED DOCTRINAL LISTS**, not inter-personal
  difference: `anusaya` = the seven-latent-defilement roster (a *universal* latency of all
  unawakened beings, Abh §156 `cst-abh02m.mul-156`, Yamaka/Kathāvatthu); `cetopariya` = the
  fixed abhiññā list-item `cetopariyañāṇa`. Counting either as person-individuation roughly
  **doubles a false early mass** → demote; cite only the small sutta subset (e.g. MN 44) for the
  person-disposition sense.
- **`paññāvimutta` 235 → ~37–44** person-type; the rest is the state-noun `paññāvimutti`.
- **`nānatta` "canon 2" is wrong (real ~159)** — neutral "diversity" word (SN 14.1
  `cst-s0302m.mul-sn2_3`), does NOT behave as an essentializing-drift marker; that leg of the
  original serendipity claim is **refuted**. `sabhāva` carries G1, not `nānatta`.
- **The "present-state vanishes upward" inverse-crossover is refuted**: `sarāgaṃ cittaṃ` persists
  into commentary (attha 4–10, tika 5–13), it does not go to 0. State only the *bloom* arm
  (disposition apparatus), not a bidirectional crossover.
- Bare stems `asay`, `neyy`, `vineyy`, `vasan`, `ninn`, `pakat`, `ruci`, `chand`, `adhimutt`,
  `indriy`, `desan`, `matik`, `gotrabh` — all over-recall into list/metaphor/suffix noise; no clean
  individuation signal at the stem level.
- Invented probe numbers (24+12=36 temperament split; defilement 16; capacity 22;
  samatha/vipassanā 30/6/6; anusaya 148) are unreproducible — import only audited figures, and
  state every count as **role-grouped**, not stem-grouped.

## 6. Proposed re-spine (function-vs-essence × stratum; carita = one worked special case)

**Phase A — per-stratum pattern-finding (earliest → latest, WIDE field).** Each stratum answers:
*by what mechanism, in what grammatical mode, under whose warrant does THIS layer individuate a
person?* Evidence stated once here.
- **A.1 Early-canon (four Nikāyas)** — the rich-early layer: present-state root-sort; the seven
  ariya-puggala liberation-mode roster; faculty-maturity; the ugghaṭitaññū receptivity tetrad;
  `puggalavemattatā` (peaks-early-declines-late); readiness (`kallacitta`, lotus-pond); the antidote
  seed + the calm-insight yoke. Internal trend: individuation by verb-of-leaning / present-participle
  / capacity-&-route rosters — never fixed temperament. Honest caveat: down-weight `anusaya`/
  `cetopariya` (doctrinal lists).
- **A.2 Abhidhamma** — typologizes persons richly under `puggala` (Pp four-types, Abh §14
  `cst-abh03m2.mul-014`) and the present-root Yamaka sort (Abh §177 `cst-abh03m5.mul-177`), plus the
  dasabala diversity-powers. `carita` still 0. Load-bearing caution: **lexical absence ≠ conceptual
  absence**.
- **A.3 Para-canonical Khuddaka** — THE HINGE: `carita`-temperament first appears (`cnd19`) while
  the older conduct-sense co-exists (`ps3.5`); a re-coinage caught mid-fork. The intra-canonical
  lateness stratum the four-Nikāya/Abhidhamma binary hides.
- **A.4 Aṭṭhakathā** — the carita grid fixed as a 6-cell person×object matrix; disposition-as-design
  vocabulary blooms (`ajjhāsaya`, `veneyya`, `asayānusaya`, `anurūpa`); the two-vehicle split
  crystallizes.
- **A.5 Ṭīkā** — temperament physiologized (heart-blood diagnostic, Vibh-a §70.58); essentialization
  peaks (`sabhāva`).

**Phase B — sweeping pattern-finding across 100% of the data (chronology-blind; evidence only
referenced back to A).**
- **B.1 Divergence** — "individuation is a late commentarial habit" is FALSE; the precise true claim
  is "fixed-TYPE individuation is late; function/state/route individuation is early." Sub-divergence:
  yoke(early) vs vehicle-split(late).
- **B.2 Addition** — the carita frame missed an entire early systematized typology (the seven
  ariya-puggala roster, ASCII-invisible; the ugghaṭitaññū ladder; the present-root Yamaka sort). The
  canon catalogues persons by route, ceiling, speed — just not by flavor/temperament.
- **B.3 Regression** — carita verdicts hold; Vism disowns its own diagnostic. Import corrected counts
  (§5).

**General-importance section (its own visible block)** — G1 essentialization drift, G2 the SN 14
homophily law, G3 the homograph trap, G4 describe-early/name-late.

## 7. What this run proves about the *method* (for later skill encoding — HELD, not yet encoded)

1. **A wide-recall discovery front-end belongs before the confirmatory machinery.** Searching the
   named term can only confirm the hypothesised pattern; horizontal recall across rival vocabularies
   is what surfaced the function-vs-essence axis and the two general finds.
2. **Per-family sense-audit is mandatory, not optional.** A high canon count of a "psychological"
   term is a category/list lexeme **until proven otherwise** (`anusaya`, `cetopariya`, `nānatta`,
   `paññāvimutta` all failed this). The carita recall ladder's discipline must recur on *every* new
   family, not just the headline one.
3. **The signature axes are the pattern substrate.** The live question fell out of cross-reading
   axes (mechanism × grammatical-mode × warrant × stratum), not from reading chronology alone.
4. **Keep a serendipity lane open by design** — the SN 14 homophily law and the sabhāva drift would
   never surface from a person-matching question; they came from an agent told to ignore it.
