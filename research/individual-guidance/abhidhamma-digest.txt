Structure-by-structure tiering (SUTTA = earliest stratum / ABHIDHAMMA = first systematizing layer, canonical / COMMENTARY = aṭṭhakathā + ṭīkā + Vism):

1. MIND-BASE (manoviññāṇa-vatthu).
   SUTTA — SILENT. No physical/material support for mind anywhere. The only proto-seed is the luminous-mind formula (AN 1.49-52, ids an1.41-50 / an1.51-60): "Pabhassaramidaṁ, bhikkhave, cittaṁ… āgantukehi upakkilesehi upakkiliṭṭhaṁ" — a moral-purity metaphor with NO base, NO mechanism.
   ABHIDHAMMA — PARTIAL / ANONYMOUS. Paṭṭhāna (patthana1.1; CST mūla cst-abh03m7.mul-002) posits a material support for the two mind-elements under nissaya-/purejāta-paccaya but leaves it un-named ("yaṁ rūpaṁ"). Dhammasaṅgaṇī Rūpa-kaṇḍa (cst-abh01m.mul-078, -077, dha.sa.584) gives a vatthu to the five sense-consciousnesses but leaves manoviññāṇa's base indeterminate/un-named.
   COMMENTARY — NAMES + PHYSICALIZES. The aṭṭhakathā on the Paṭṭhāna (cst-abh03a.att-370_p002) substitutes hadaya-vatthu (the heart) for the bare "rūpa." The Abhidhamma-ṭīkā (cst-abh07t.nrf-135_p014) and Vism-mahāṭīkā (cst-e0104n.att-16_p024) define it, locate it in the physical heart, cite Paṭṭhāna 1.1.8 as āgama, and flag that Dhammasaṅgaṇī 584 never stated it; a further ṭīkā (cst-abh04t.nrf-67_p005) explicitly notes the Abhidhamma did not take it up.

2. BHAVAṄGA (life-continuum).
   SUTTA — ABSENT. Nearest analogue is again the luminous mind (an1.41-50); no life-continuum.
   ABHIDHAMMA — PRESENT (as a bare term). Paṭṭhāna (patthana2.1, patthana1.3, CST mūla cst-abh03m9.mul-071) uses bhavaṅga as a real canonical relatum in Anantara-/Samanantara-paccaya (and Vedanā-/Pīti-/Atītārammaṇa-ttika) — but only as a discrete cognitive state, with no series and no heart.
   COMMENTARY — BUILDS THE MACHINE. The ṭīkā (cst-abh01t.tik-69_p013) ties bhavaṅga to hadaya-vatthu as an object-less life-continuum; the Abhidhammattha-saṅgaha manual layer (cst-e0301n.nrf-008) builds the citta-vīthi vocabulary (bhavaṅga-calana, the disturbed/arrested continuum) — the processual model the Paṭṭhāna never states.

3. CARITA TYPOLOGY (six temperaments: rāga/dosa/moha/saddhā/buddhi/vitakka).
   SUTTA — ABSENT (in six-fold form).
   ABHIDHAMMA — ABSENT in six-fold form, and the word "carita" means something else: Vibhaṅga Ñāṇavibhaṅga (cst-abh02m.mul-149, §817) glosses carita kammically (three abhisaṅkhāra), NOT temperament. The Puggalapaññatti (cst-abh03m2.mul-014) carries a large person-typology (four understanding-types ugghaṭitaññū/vipañcitaññū/neyya/padaparama §148-151 + ~20 four-fold simile/conduct types) but NOT the six caritas.
   PARA-CANON (intermediate) — THREE-FOLD ROOT-CARITA + REMEDY. Nettippakaraṇa (ne6) and Sāsanapaṭṭhāna (cst-s0519m.mul-kn19_6) already carry a three-fold rāga/dosa/moha-carita with the asubha/mettā/paṭiccasamuppāda remedy matrix and combinatorial permutations — but strictly on the three roots.
   COMMENTARY — SIX-FOLD PERSONALITY TYPING. Aṭṭhakathā (cst-s0201a.att-mn1_3_p261) first gives the six-fold temperament typology; the Abhidhamma-ṭīkā (cst-abh07t.nrf-70_p002) fixes it as a "chabbidha caritasaṅgaha"; Vism III supplies the affinity theory (cst-e0101n.mul-36_p003) and the carita→object/dwelling/robe/bowl/food/posture/kammaṭṭhāna suitability matrix plus heart-blood/gait/eating diagnostics (cst-e0101n.mul-36_p031).

Per Abhidhamma-rooted structure, the precise commentarial surplus:

MIND-BASE — the Abhidhamma supplies an anonymous material support ("yaṁ rūpaṁ," Paṭṭhāna) and an indeterminate manoviññāṇa-vatthu (Dhammasaṅgaṇī 584). The commentary adds (1) the heart-NAME: it substitutes hadaya-vatthu into the bare "rūpa," explicitly self-aware that it is doing so — quoting Paṭṭhāna 1.1.8 as its āgama-authority for existence while citing Dhammasaṅgaṇī 584 as the place the heart was withheld; and (2) the physicalization — locating the base in the physical heart-chamber (the half-handful of blood). It names the organ the Abhidhamma left blank.

BHAVAṄGA — the Abhidhamma supplies bhavaṅga as a real but bare term, a discrete relatum in the Paṭṭhāna's conditional-relations method (Anantara/Samanantara-paccaya), with no process and no location. The commentary adds (1) the citta-vīthi machinery — the full cognitive series (bhavaṅga-calana, the vibrating/arrested continuum disturbed by a sense-object) absent from the Paṭṭhāna; and (2) the heart-tie — bhavaṅga reconceived as an object-less life-continuum running on hadaya-vatthu. The Abhidhamma gives the part; the commentary assembles the machine and seats it in the heart.

CARITA — the Abhidhamma supplies the three roots (akusalamūla: rāga/dosa/moha, Vibhaṅga + Dhammasaṅgaṇī) but uses the word "carita" only kammically (three abhisaṅkhāra, NOT temperament), and its person-typology (Puggalapaññatti four understanding-types) is not carita-based. The para-canon then supplies a three-fold root-carita + remedy matrix. The commentary adds (a) the EXPANSION of three caritas to six by appending saddhā/buddhi/vitakka; (b) the affinity theory justifying the pairing (rāga↔saddhā, dosa↔buddhi, moha↔vitakka, Vism III); (c) the carita→object/dwelling/robe/bowl/almsround/food/posture/kammaṭṭhāna suitability matrix; and (d) the heart-blood-colour / gait / eating diagnostics of the Vism Cariyāvaṇṇanā.

NAMED INSIGHT-ÑĀṆAS — the Abhidhamma does NOT contain them at all (negative controls); the para-canonical Paṭisambhidāmagga first defines them. So relative to the Abhidhamma the commentary's contribution is total for the staging, but relative to its real source (the Pm) the commentary adds the refinements — e.g. the momentary-vs-continuity (khaṇato/santativasena) analysis of udayabbayañāṇa that the plain Pm niddesa leaves unstated — and the integration of the named ñāṇas into the seven-purification frame.

SEVEN PURIFICATIONS — the skeleton is SUTTA (MN 24), not Abhidhamma; relative to the Abhidhamma the commentary adds the entire graded weave: it populates the bare seven-list with Abhidhamma analytical objects and slots the Pm's named knowledges into the maggāmagga + paṭipadā purifications, and self-describes the architecture (root/body division operating on the khandha-āyatana-dhātu-paṭiccasamuppāda bhūmi).

KHANDHA/ĀYATANA/DHĀTU/PAṬICCASAMUPPĀDA — the Abhidhamma (Vibhaṅga) is the canonical source of the dissection itself; the commentary adds no new categories but supplies the operation — using them as the "ground" the insight ladder marks anicca/dukkha/anattā and dissects.

[ABHIDHAMMA_STATUS_NOTE]
Two positions stand opposed, and the choice between them is what forces the three-tier axis.

TRADITIONAL THERAVĀDA view: the seven Abhidhamma books are full Buddhavacana. The Buddha is held to be the first Abhidhammika, to have contemplated the seven books in the fourth week after awakening, then to have preached the Abhidhamma for three uninterrupted months in Tāvatiṃsa heaven to the devas headed by his mother (reborn as the deva Santusita), returning daily to convey the method to Sāriputta, who transmitted it onward. The decisive point for tiering: this origin-narrative is itself NOT in the suttas — it is a COMMENTARIAL account, principally in Buddhaghosa's Atthasālinī (the commentary to the first Abhidhamma book). By its own provenance the story that authorizes the Abhidhamma as Buddhavacana sits a tier ABOVE the canon it explains.

CRITICAL SCHOLARSHIP view: the seven books are LATER than the four Nikāyas (composed/redacted c. 3rd c. BCE – 2nd c. CE) and so are the work of later disciples, not the Buddha's direct words. The Kathāvatthu is dated to the Asokan era, traditionally ascribed to Moggaliputta-tissa at the Third Council (c. 250 BCE) — an ascription itself contested as a later Mahāvihāra interpolation. The seed of the Abhidhamma is the mātikā (the doctrinal matrix/list) already embedded in the suttas; Frauwallner, Warder, Cousins, and Gethin trace the literature's growth out of those lists. On this view the Abhidhamma is itself a proto-systematization — the BRIDGE between sutta and commentary — so the suttas remain the earliest stratum while the Abhidhamma is the first systematizing layer, not an independent earliest source.

IMPLICATION for this study: because the Theravāda canon formally INCLUDES the Abhidhamma, a rigorous canon-vs-commentary analysis cannot use a flat two-tier (canon / commentary) split. It needs a three-tier axis — sutta / abhidhamma / commentary — in which the Abhidhamma is canonical but second, and the commentary (including the very Tāvatiṃsa account that sacralizes the Abhidhamma) is third.

How this revises the original individual-guidance (canon-vs-commentary) study:

1. The criterion-by-layer contrast becomes THREE-tier, not two. Every structure must be scored against sutta / abhidhamma / commentary rather than canon / commentary. The flat split mislabels the load-bearing middle cases: the mind-base, bhavaṅga, the three roots, and the analytical categories are all CANONICAL (Abhidhamma) yet ABSENT from the suttas — a two-tier scheme either wrongly files them as "canon = same as sutta" or wrongly credits the commentary with their invention. The corpus shows four distinct loci (sutta / abhidhamma / para-canon / commentary), with the para-canon (Nettippakaraṇa, Peṭakopadesa, Paṭisambhidāmagga) acting as the intermediate bridge for the carita-remedy idea and the named ñāṇas.

2. The carita hypothesis H1 sharpens from "carita = commentarial" to: ROOTS ABHIDHAMMA, TYPING COMMENTARIAL. The rāga/dosa/moha roots are canonical Abhidhamma (Vibhaṅga, Dhammasaṅgaṇī); the three-fold root-carita + remedy matrix is PARA-CANON (Nettippakaraṇa); and only the SIX-FOLD personality typology — the saddhā/buddhi/vitakka expansion, the affinity theory, and the diagnostic/prescriptive suitability matrix — is the commentarial innovation (Aṭṭhakathā / Abhidhamma-ṭīkā / Vism III). Note too that the Abhidhamma's own word "carita" means kamma-formations, not temperament — so the temperament sense is itself a commentarial re-coinage.

3. Bhavaṅga and the mind-base are reclassified from pure commentarial INVENTIONS to Abhidhamma-canonical commentarial ELABORATIONS. Both terms exist in the canonical Abhidhamma (bhavaṅga as a bare relatum in the Paṭṭhāna; the mind-base as an anonymous "rūpa" in the Paṭṭhāna and an indeterminate vatthu in Dhammasaṅgaṇī). What the commentary genuinely adds is therefore narrower and more precise: (a) the heart-NAME (hadaya-vatthu) substituted for the Abhidhamma's anonymous matter, plus its physicalization; and (b) the citta-vīthi / bhavaṅga-as-life-continuum machinery seated on that heart-base. The true commentarial adds are the heart-NAME and the cognitive series — not the existence of the base or of bhavaṅga.

4. General correction to the study's authority framing: the Abhidhamma's own claim to Buddhavacana rests on a commentarial origin-account (Atthasālinī's Tāvatiṃsa narrative), so the study should treat Abhidhamma-canonicity as canonical-but-second and should not let the Abhidhamma's formal canonical status collapse it into the sutta tier.
