# How a Person Is Matched to a Practice, Under the Provenance Signature: The Carita Apparatus as a Post-Canonical Stratum, and a Word That Changed Its Job

## Abstract

An earlier study of how the Pali tradition guides an individual toward awakening read the material on one cross-cutting axis, canon versus commentary, and reached a defensible verdict: the canon keys a meditation object to a person's present defilement or situation, while the famous apparatus of six temperaments (carita), the closed set of forty meditation subjects, and the technical sense of kammatthana belong to the commentaries. That verdict is true on its axis. Recoded under the provenance signature, which separates structural layer from chronological stratum, from epistemic marking, and from cross-recensional reach, the verdict holds and sharpens into a chronology fact with three new edges. First, the matching apparatus is not merely commentarial in voice; it is post-canonical in time. The temperament-sense carita compound (ragacarita, dosacarita, and the rest) is attested zero times in the four Nikayas and zero times in the seven Abhidhamma books. Its earliest carrier is the para-canonical Khuddaka analytical layer, the Culaniddesa, and it then hardens through the Visuddhimagga and the sub-commentary. Of the fifty-three structurally mula rows that carry the compound, every one is Visuddhimagga or para-canonical Khuddaka; not one is from the four Nikayas or the Abhidhamma. Second, the word itself moved. Tracked across strata, carita drifts from a verb of motion and an ethical compound in early-canon prose, through a para-canonical fork where the Patisambhidamagga keeps the old conduct-sense (eight cariya, a taxonomy of modes-of-activity) while the Niddesa mints the new temperament-sense, into a fixed commentarial typology, into a sub-commentarial somatic diagnostic that reads temperament off the colour of the heart-blood, and out into a modern psychology of personality types. The temperament sense is a re-coinage, not a canonical given. Third, the matching system is systematized, never asserted-verified. Where the typology and a knowing-formula co-occur in mula rows the gaps run to tens and hundreds of thousands of characters, a proximity artifact, and the one tight co-occurrence is the Visuddhimagga's own disclaimer that its method of determining temperament has come down neither in the canon nor in the old commentary, is stated only following the teachers' opinion, and is not to be relied on as authoritative. The canonical seed the apparatus is built on, the defilement-to-object antidote formula and the yoke of calm and insight, is pre-sectarian, attested in the Madhyama and Samyukta Agamas; the carita typology welded onto it is, on the linked evidence, Pali-local. Materialisation and verification are different axes here too, and the apparatus that grew most elaborate is the one the tradition itself declined to certify.

## The corrected headline

The original study scored each assignment for whether a canonical row warranted it and concluded that a canon of defilement-and-situation antidotes was systematized by a commentary of temperament-and-object matrices. That axis is preserved here as the first row of a fuller signature. What it could not see, because the mula tag is a position in the edited corpus and not a date, is that the commentarial apparatus is not chronologically flat against the canon: it is a late stratum whose seed is early and whose superstructure is post-canonical, and the key term that names it changed sense on the way.

The corrected headline is this. A canonical seed, the keying of a meditation object to a present defilement (foulness for lust, loving-kindness for ill will, mindfulness of breathing for discursive thought, the perception of impermanence for the conceit "I am"), is early-canonical and pre-sectarian, stated in AN 9.1 and Ud 4.1 and surviving in the Agamas. On that seed the tradition builds a person-classification, the six caritas, that has no attestation in the four Nikayas and none in the Abhidhamma. The classification enters first in the para-canonical Khuddaka, in the Culaniddesa, where it is ascribed to the Buddha's knowing; it is fixed as a six-cell person-by-object matrix in the Visuddhimagga and the atthakatha; it is given a somatic diagnostic in the sub-commentary; and it is read, in modern translation, through the category of personality type. At no point in this growth does the tradition stake the matching under its own formula for verified knowledge. The Visuddhimagga, the very manual that fixes the apparatus, flags its temperament diagnostics as merely the teachers' opinion and not authoritative.

Three things follow that the single axis could not produce. The systematization the earlier study credited to "the commentary" is better dated than voiced: it is a post-canonical layer with a para-canonical hinge, and "commentarial" understates how the seed predates the split while the apparatus postdates the canon. The word carita carries a semantic drift, from conduct to temperament to diagnosable type, that a flat layer split renders invisible. And the apparatus is epistemically weak by the tradition's own marking: the more elaborate the matching machinery becomes, the more explicitly the tradition declines to certify it.

## Methodology

### The provenance signature and the triage

The method replaces the single canon-versus-commentary axis with a provenance signature, a small triaged set of independent dimensions. The full framework carries eleven axes; the triage maps the question's load-bearing claims to the axes they actually depend on, because coding every axis on every instance would bury the study.

A scope gate runs first. The question this study can answer is descriptive, not normative. "How is a person matched to a teaching or object" is a question about what the texts license and how carita is assigned; "what meditation object should an angry temperament use" is a normative practice question the corpus cannot adjudicate and the tradition itself makes teacher-assigned, not self-diagnosed. The descriptive reading is the one coded throughout, and the normative gap is flagged rather than filled.

On that descriptive question the load-bearing axes are four. Chronological stratum (I.1), because the whole prior finding rests on a canon-versus-commentary contrast, and if the canonical instances of the apparatus are themselves post-canonical the contrast is really a within-corpus chronology. The semantic-drift strip (the mandated output of I.1 when a single term is tracked across strata), because carita's temperament sense was already flagged as a re-coinage and the strip is what makes that a measured artifact rather than an aside. Epistemic marking (II.7), because the question of whether the matching is asserted-verified or merely systematized turns on the speech-act status the texts assign it. And cross-recensional reach (I.4), because the question of whether the typology is pre-sectarian or Theravada-local is exactly the antiquity question that Pali-internal dating cannot settle alone. The always-on core, the structural layer-and-voice bit and the recall ladder, runs underneath all four. The axes coded only where they bear are genre and register, attribution, and the reception overlay (which the drift strip's modern-translation column reuses). Pre-Buddhist provenance and the harmonization axis are not load-bearing here and are not coded: the apparatus is a Buddhist scholastic construction with no obvious pan-Indian antecedent to inherit, and the tradition flags the apparatus as unauthoritative rather than working to reconcile it with a canonical tension, so the reconciliation machinery the harmonization axis tracks does not fire.

### The recall ladder

Every claim rests on a measured enumeration. The key term carita is a trap twice over, and the ladder is what disarms it.

The first rung, the naive surface token, is near-empty: carita is almost always compounded, so a word-boundary search for the bare token returns five rows. The second rung, the morphological stem carit, over-collects in the opposite direction: it returns 824 mula rows, but the stem also catches the verb carita and the past participle, so the raw stem count conflates the temperament noun with unrelated forms. The third rung is the load-bearing one: the sense-disambiguated temperament compound, the stem prefixed by a defilement (raga-, dosa-, moha-, vitakka-, saddha-, nana-carita), grouped by work. This returns fifty-three mula rows, and the distribution is the finding: thirty Visuddhimagga, ten in the analytical Khuddaka, four Nettippakarana, three Niddesa, three Petakopadesa, two Milindapanha, one Patisambhidamagga, and zero in each of the four Nikayas and zero in the Abhidhamma. The fourth rung is the concept-independent pass, searching the canonical seed without the name: the antidote formula asubha bhavetabba ragassa pahanaya and its companions returns thirty-five mula rows, five of them in the Anguttara and one in the Udana. The defilement-to-object function is canonical without the carita name; the typology is the post-canonical overlay on that seed.

Every load-bearing count below was reconfirmed by a direct database count grouped by textual role, not by the search lane. The ladder grows from five to a sense-disambiguated fifty-three (mula) and is reported in full so the residual recall risk is visible. The gap between the naive rung and the disambiguated rung is itself the methodological point: a substring search for "carita" would have either missed the compounds or drowned them in the verb.

## The within-canon stratigraphy: the apparatus is a post-canonical layer

The apparatus the prior study called commentarial resolves, under chronological stratum, into three elements that all code post-canonical, each with a structural layer that disagrees with its stratum.

| The six-carita temperament typology | para-canonical, then commentary | mula-tagged + commentary | 0 | yes |
| kammatthana in the technical "meditation-subject" sense | classical-commentary | mula-tagged (Visuddhimagga) | 0 (technical sense) | yes |
| The closed set of forty meditation subjects | classical-commentary | mula-tagged (Visuddhimagga) | 0 | yes |

**The six-carita typology.** The temperament-sense compound is attested zero times in the four Nikayas and zero times in the Abhidhamma. Its fifty-three mula attestations are all Visuddhimagga (thirty) or para-canonical Khuddaka (the Niddesa, Nettippakarana, Petakopadesa, Patisambhidamagga, Milindapanha). The earliest full matrix is the Culaniddesa, glossing the Mogharaja verse: *Janati bhagava, "ayam puggalo ragacarito, ayam dosacarito, ayam mohacarito, ayam vitakkacarito, ayam saddhacarito, ayam nanacarito"ti. Ragacaritassa bhagava puggalassa asubhakatham katheti; dosacaritassa ... mettabhavanam acikkhati*, "The Blessed One knows: 'this person is of greed-temperament, this of hate-, delusion-, discursive-thought-, faith-, knowledge-temperament.' To the greed-temperament person the Blessed One teaches the talk on foulness; to the hate-temperament he points out the development of loving-kindness" (`cnd19`) [canon, para-canonical]. The structural layer is mula; the stratum is para-canonical Khuddaka; they disagree, and the disagreement is the finding.

> *Per-claim signature: [stratum: para-canonical (Culaniddesa) hardening to classical-commentary | layer: mula-tagged + commentary | speaker: redactor-frame ascribing the typology to the Buddha's knowing | register: scholastic-analytical | epistemic: flat assertion, never under a verification formula | recension: feature pali-only-unconfirmed | edition: cst-burmese]. Warrant: cnd19.* [canon, para-canonical]

**kammatthana in the technical sense.** Of 148 mula rows carrying the word, 134 are the Visuddhimagga. The handful in the Anguttara, Majjhima, and Digha carry the ordinary sense, a livelihood or place of work (gharavasa-kammatthana, the household occupation), not a meditation subject. The technical sense is effectively confined to the Visuddhimagga, which confirms and quantifies the reading of Crosby, Skilton, and Kyaw (2019) and corrects an overstatement in earlier work of ours that had claimed zero canonical hits: the word is canonical, the technical sense is not. The Visuddhimagga's own chapter heading is the marker: *Cattalisakammatthanavannana* (`cst-e0101n.mul-37_p001`) [comm].

**The closed forty.** The enumerated set of forty meditation subjects, *tatrimani cattalisa kammatthanani, dasa kasina, dasa asubha* and the rest, occurs in exactly two mula rows, both the Visuddhimagga's own Cattalisakammatthana chapter (`cst-e0101n.mul-37_p003`) [comm]. It appears nowhere in the four Nikayas or the Abhidhamma. That the Vimuttimagga preserved in Chinese counts thirty-eight, not forty (Bapat 1937), is the standing reminder that the closed forty is a settling, not a given; the number is a scholastic decision, and a different manual in a sibling line drew it differently.

### What the stratigraphy licenses, and what it does not

Two honesty guards bound the claim. First, "para-canonical" and "classical-commentary" are relative-stratum codes on consensus Pali philological grounds (Niddesa and Patisambhidamagga as analytical Khuddaka later than the four-Nikaya prose; the Visuddhimagga as Buddhaghosa-era redaction), not absolute dates. Second, and unlike the Uttarakuru case, the stratum codes here are not at risk of the genre-as-date circularity in their load-bearing form, because the decisive evidence is not a register label but a hard lexical absence reconfirmed by SQL: the temperament-carita compound is simply not in the four Nikayas or the Abhidhamma, in any register. A genre label is doing no work in "zero attestations in the Nikayas." The one place register could be mistaken for date is the reading of the Nikaya seed (the antidote formula) as "early", and that rests on its cross-recensional attestation, not on its register, so the chronology and recension axes corroborate rather than circle.

## The carita semantic-drift strip: a word that changed its job

The clearest single object in this retrofit is the drift of carita across strata. Each cell below records the term's sense at that stratum with a verbatim anchor drawn from that same stratum; no early cell is filled from a later one.

| Stratum | Sense of carita / cariya | Anchor (verbatim, from this stratum) |
|---|---|---|
| Early-canon prose (four Nikayas) | Absent as a noun of temperament. The cognates are the verb carati ("courses, conducts oneself") and the ethical compounds sucarita / duccarita ("good / bad conduct"). | The antidote formula carries the function without the term: *asubha bhavetabba ragassa pahanaya ... metta bhavetabba byapadassa pahanaya ... anapanassati bhavetabba vitakkupacchedaya* (`an9.1`, `ud4.1`) |
| Late / para-canonical Khuddaka | Two senses co-exist. The Patisambhidamagga keeps "conduct / coursing": eight cariya, a taxonomy of modes-of-activity. The Culaniddesa mints the temperament-sense matrix. | PS 3.5: *Cariyati, attha cariyayo, iriyapathacariya, ayatanacariya, saticariya, samadhicariya, nanacariya, maggacariya, patticariya, lokatthacariya* (`ps3.5`); CND 19: *ayam puggalo ragacarito ... Ragacaritassa bhagava ... asubhakatham katheti* (`cnd19`) |
| Atthakatha + Visuddhimagga | A fixed temperament-typology, welded to objects, with assignment machinery. | Sv-a 11 sec.2: *Sattha tesam cariyavasena ragacaritassa asubhakammatthanam deti. Dosacaritassa mettakammatthanam ...* (`cst-s0103a.att-dn3_11_p002`) |
| Tika | Temperament physiologized, read off the body. | Vibh-a sec.70.58: *Tam panetam ragacaritassa rattam hoti, dosacaritassa kalakam, mohacaritassa mamsadhovanudakasadisam ...* (`cst-abh02a.att-70_p058`) |
| Modern translation | Read through the modern category of a stable, diagnosable "personality / character type". | carita < carati; the gloss "temperament / personality type" is the reception frame, not the Pali sense |

The drift points, classified:

1. **Early-canon prose to late-canonical (DRIFT-reframing).** The function (keying an object to a defilement) is canonical, but the noun carita is re-coined for a new job. In the para-canonical layer it first names a fixed temperament (Culaniddesa) alongside its older conduct sense (Patisambhidamagga). A new sense is minted on an old word.

2. **Late-canonical to atthakatha (DRIFT-narrowing-specification).** The two co-existing late-canonical senses collapse to one. The commentary keeps only the temperament reading, fixes it as a six-cell person-by-object matrix, and builds the assignment machinery; the conduct sense is dropped for this purpose.

3. **Atthakatha to tika (DRIFT-enrichment).** The temperament sense is enriched with somatic diagnostics. Carita is now read off the body, the heart-blood colour keyed to type, an added physiological layer with no canonical or atthakatha-formula warrant.

4. **Tika to modern translation (DRIFT-reframing).** The Pali carita ("that which one habitually courses in") is reframed by the modern category "personality / character type", importing a psychology of stable diagnosable types the Pali term does not carry.

The Patisambhidamagga cell is the hinge that makes the drift legible. In the same para-canonical stratum where the temperament-matrix is born, the Cariyakatha uses cariya to mean exactly the older thing, a mode of conduct: posture-conduct, sense-base conduct, mindfulness-conduct, and the rest. The word had not yet committed to "temperament" even in the layer that first deployed it that way. That is a re-coinage caught mid-fork, not a stable canonical sense the commentary merely elaborated.

## The epistemic axis: systematized, not asserted-verified

The most consequential thing the tradition does with the matching apparatus is decline to certify it. The whole question of whether the carita-matching is asserted-verified or merely systematized turns on whether the texts ever stake it under their own formula for verified knowledge, and the answer is that they do not, and that the Visuddhimagga says so in as many words.

The canonical agency claim, registered as H_A in the prior study, is that identifying who should receive what is a Buddha's perceptual faculty: the knowledge of the maturity of others' faculties (indriyaparopariya-nana, MN 12) and the knowledge of beings' dispositions and latent tendencies (asayanusaya-nana). Those faculties are staked under a knowing-formula, but their object in the canon is capacity and latent tendency, and the four Nikayas attach no six-carita typology to them. The faculty that is verified reads something the typology is not.

Where the typology and a knowing-formula do co-occur in mula rows, the co-occurrence is a proximity artifact, not a verification. The temperament term and the knowing-faculty sit tens or hundreds of thousands of characters apart in the same monolithic Niddesa and Nettippakarana rows (gaps of 16,852, 132,840, and 229,586 characters), in wholly separate pericopes. This is the same structured-absence pattern the Uttarakuru study reports for the divine eye: a formula present in the volume, never in the window of the claim.

The one tight co-occurrence is decisive, and it is the tradition's own disclaimer. The Visuddhimagga, immediately after surveying how a teacher might read a pupil's temperament from gait, posture, eating, and the rest, stops and says: *Yasma pana idam cariyavibhavanavidhanam sabbakarena neva paliyam na atthakathayam agatam, kevalam acariyamatanusarena vuttam, tasma na sarato paccetabbam*, "But because this method of determining temperament has come down, in all its aspects, neither in the canon nor in the [old] commentary, and is stated only following the teachers' opinion, it is not to be relied on as authoritative" (`cst-e0101n.mul-36_p030`) [comm]. The matching is staked, at most, under a conditional supernormal knowing: a teacher who has the attainment of mind-reading (cetopariyanana) will know the temperament and assign accordingly; failing that, the pupil is simply to be questioned. The apex manual of the tradition fixes the apparatus and in the same breath marks it unauthoritative.

> *Per-claim signature (the keystone): Claim "the carita-to-object matching is verified knowledge". Status: rejected by the tradition's own marking. [stratum: classical-commentary | layer: mula-tagged Visuddhimagga | speaker: commentary-voice, self-flagging | register: scholastic-analytical | epistemic: explicitly non-authoritative (na sarato paccetabbam); at most conditional cetopariyanana, otherwise questioning | recension: pali-only | edition: cst-burmese].* [comm]

The bound, stated honestly: the apparatus is systematized, not asserted-verified, and the tradition itself draws that line. This does not license the stronger claim that the tradition rejected the typology; it kept it, taught it, and elaborated it. It licenses the precise claim that the matching machinery is held as serviceable scholastic guidance, not as certified knowledge, and that the most elaborate part of it, the temperament diagnostics, is the part the tradition explicitly refuses to certify.

## The cross-recensional split: a pre-sectarian seed, a Pali-local typology

The antiquity question, whether the matching apparatus is pre-sectarian or Theravada-local, is not settled by Pali-internal stratum. The bridge is the corpus's parallels table, and the container-versus-feature distinction is kept sharp.

The canonical seed is pre-sectarian. The defilement-to-object antidote formula of AN 9.1 and Ud 4.1 has Madhyama Agama parallels (`ma56`, `ma57`) and Udanavarga witnesses; the yoke of calm and insight, AN 4.170 Yuganaddha, has a Samyukta Agama parallel (`sa560`); the six recollections that furnish the faith-cell objects, AN 6.10, have Samyukta Agama parallels. The functional core the apparatus is built on, keying practice to defilement and developing calm and insight together, survives in lines independent of the Pali and plausibly predates the sectarian split.

The carita typology, by contrast, is Pali-local on the linked evidence, and the code is held unconfirmed at feature level. The Culaniddesa row that carries the matrix does have external parallels, but those parallels are to the Mogharaja verse the Niddesa is glossing, not to the carita-typology feature; this is the container-versus-feature trap, and the feature is not in the parallel. The typology's genuine non-Pali witness is not a sutta at all but the Vimuttimagga of Upatissa, preserved only in Chinese, which carries a cognate temperament scheme; a sutta-to-sutta parallels table does not and cannot register it. So the correct code is pali-only-pending-feature-level-verification, never "Theravada-invented": the Vimuttimagga's cognate scheme makes the typology plausibly pre-Buddhaghosa, though it remains post-canonical, and a feature-level check on the Vimuttimagga and any Sarvastivada or Yogacarabhumi temperament material would harden or dissolve the local-versus-shared question.

## The regression check: the prior verdicts hold

Two prior verdicts were re-asserted from the same census rather than restated, so a silent drift would have failed the build. The three-tier headline is unchanged: of the kept instances, 46 stand in the sutta tier, 1 in the Abhidhamma, 4 in the para-canonical bridge, and 212 in the commentary, and the H0/H1 split over the fifteen decidable assignment cells stands at eight warranted to seven unwarranted, "faithful in principle, innovative in apparatus."

The samatha-and-vipassana verdict also holds and is sharpened by the same chronology lens that carries this retrofit. The canon presents calm and insight yoked: yuganaddha is robustly mula (thirty-five rows), and across the discourses the census reached, insight is never assigned alone, as a standing practice, to a named person. The two-vehicle split (the samathayanika and vipassanayanika division) and the dry-insight worker (sukkhavipassaka) are overwhelmingly commentarial (three and three mula rows respectively against tens of commentarial ones), and every one of those few mula rows is, once again, the Visuddhimagga, structurally mula-tagged but classical-commentary in stratum. The dry-insight vehicle is not merely commentarial in voice; it is, like the carita apparatus, a Visuddhimagga-era stratum wearing a mula tag. This confirms and dates Cousins's reading.

## Discussion

The corrected headline is sharper than its predecessor, and the sharpening comes from refusing to let the structural axis stand for the whole provenance question. On the structural axis the apparatus reads as a commentarial systematization of a canonical core. That sentence is true, and it conceals three facts that change its meaning.

First, "commentarial" is a voice label standing in for a date. The carita typology, the technical kammatthana, the closed forty, and the dry-insight vehicle are not merely in the commentary; they are a post-canonical stratum, with a para-canonical hinge in the Niddesa for the temperament typology and a Visuddhimagga-era body for the rest, sitting against a canonical seed that is itself pre-sectarian. The contrast the prior study drew between canon and commentary is, more precisely, a contrast between a pre-sectarian functional seed and a post-canonical scholastic superstructure, and the structural tag hides both the antiquity of the seed and the lateness of the superstructure.

Second, the word that names the apparatus changed its job, and the drift is the cleanest evidence that the typology is a construction rather than a canonical given. Carita travels from a verb of conduct and an ethical compound, through a para-canonical fork where the older conduct-sense and the new temperament-sense sit side by side in the same stratum, into a fixed commentarial typology, into a somatic diagnostic, and out into a modern psychology of types. A study that coded only structural layer would see the same word in canon and commentary and miss that it is not the same word.

Third, the apparatus is epistemically weak by the tradition's own marking, and weakest exactly where it is most elaborate. The canon stakes its knowing-formula on capacity and latent tendency, not on a temperament typology; the typology, when it finally meets a knowing-formula in the Visuddhimagga, meets a disclaimer. The matching is serviceable scholastic guidance, held as the teachers' opinion and not to be relied on as authoritative, and the somatic diagnostics that read temperament off the body are the part the tradition itself flags. This is the descriptive answer to the question the scope gate insisted on: the texts license a teacher-assigned, openly provisional matching, never a self-diagnosed or certified one.

The methodological lesson repeats the one the Uttarakuru retrofit drew. "Canon versus commentary" is one projection of a richer object, and for any question whose load rests on when a formulation entered, how the tradition marks its certainty, and how original it is across the recensions, the single projection misleads in a predictable direction: it credits to a flat "commentary" a stratum that is both later than the canon and, in its seed, older than the schools, and it reads a re-coined term as though it had one stable sense. The chronological, epistemic, and cross-recensional axes recover what the structural tag flattens.

## Limitations

The chronological stratification is relative, not absolute. It rests on consensus Pali philological grounds (the analytical Khuddaka as later than the four-Nikaya prose, the Visuddhimagga as a fifth-century redaction of older Sihala material), imports no absolute dates, and is anchored where it is strongest: the load-bearing chronology claim is a hard lexical absence (zero temperament-carita in the four Nikayas and zero in the Abhidhamma) reconfirmed by SQL, not a register label converted into a date. The reading of the seed as "early" rests on its cross-recensional attestation, not on its register, so the chronology and recension axes corroborate rather than circle.

The cross-recensional finding is link-level, not feature-level. "No linked parallel" for the carita typology is evidence about the corpus's sutta-to-sutta parallels table, not affirmative evidence about the Agama and Abhidharma traditions; the typology's real non-Pali witness, the Vimuttimagga, is a manual the table cannot register, and a parallel of the most relevant kind would sit in the cosmology-and-meditation-manual genre the sutta table under-covers. The code is pali-only-pending-feature-level-verification, never "original" or "Theravada-invented", and the pre-sectarian status of the seed is asserted only where an Agama witness was affirmatively linked.

The character-gap evidence for the epistemic absence is edition-relative. The gaps are computed over the CST monolithic rows as ingested; under a finer segmentation the absolute numbers change. What does not change is the structural fact the gaps stand for, that the knowing-formula and the carita typology never share a pericope-window, and that the Visuddhimagga's own disclaimer is edition-neutral.

The recall ceiling is the assignment vocabulary. An assignment phrased in terms the frame did not cover could be missed; within the tradition's own closed lists (the four understanding-types, the antidote formula, the six temperaments, the forty objects) the enumeration is saturated, but saturation here measures structural completeness, not a proof that the open corpus holds no further instance.

The analytic vocabulary is a reception layer. "Temperament", "typology", "personality type", and "diagnostic" are modern frames laid over the Pali, named as such throughout, and the modern-translation cell of the drift strip is the explicit statement of that frame. Where the corpus carries no English the rendering is the present author's gloss checked against the standard translations, with load-bearing terms quoted in Pali.
