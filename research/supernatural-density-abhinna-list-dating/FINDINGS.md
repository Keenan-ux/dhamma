# Where the supernatural lives: a density test for a hardening seam in the six-abhinna list

## Abstract

This study asked a corpus-linguistic question: does the DISPLAY register of the supernatural (the iddhi-patihariya label, the iddhividha performance catalogue, the yamaka-patihariya) grow denser per character from early canon into commentary, while a within-author DIAGNOSTIC control (the iddhipada path factor) stays flat, and does the polysemous word abhinna harden from an open WIDE sense into the closed six-item NARROW list? The verdict is null. The primary test could not be run: the two queries that resolve the DISPLAY density slope both hit a database statement timeout, so DISPLAY per-character density is unmeasured. The supporting queries that did run cut against the house thesis rather than rescuing it, and the negative control did not stay flat. Per the program rule that a finding stands only if its negative control did not move with it, the result is reported as a null with the artifact arm of the pre-committed H0 gaining. No datable hardening seam is established at corpus-linguistic resolution.

## The question and why it is a fair test

The house thesis (H1) is that the canon is supple and the commentary systematizes. Operationally H1 made four falsifiable predictions: (a) DISPLAY per-character density rises from early canon into commentary by a clear margin; (b) the DISPLAY slope exceeds the DIAGNOSTIC slope; (c) the NARROW (fixed six-list) sense-share of abhinna increases from early canon to commentary; (d) the chalabhinna fixed collocation appears late and densifies into commentary as a visible seam. The test is fair because it was pre-committed against a named null (H0), it counts per character rather than by raw rows, it carries a within-register control (iddhipada, predicted flat) and two neutral negative controls (dukkha primary, paticca robustness), and it gates the abhinna sense-split on inter-annotator agreement before the NARROW-share numbers are allowed to stand. Stratum here is a work_slug lookup, not a dated time axis; no statement below is chronological evidence.

## What the canon shows

The primary axis is missing. Query 01_display_density_by_stratum and query 03_display_components_split both returned "canceling statement due to statement timeout" and produced no rows. DISPLAY per-character density is therefore unmeasured, and predictions H1(a) and H1(b) are untested. The verdict is null by non-execution on the primary axis, not null after a clean DISPLAY measurement.

The within-register DIAGNOSTIC control (query 02) is roughly flat across canon and commentary: per-Mchar 1early 5.803, 3abh 5.675, 5comm 5.572, 6tika 5.325, with a single small-N 2late spike to 11.942 (only 46 rows over 3.85 Mchar). The commentary-to-early ratio is 0.960, essentially no rise. The DIAGNOSTIC register, the one part of the iddhi vocabulary that should be stable, is stable.

## What the commentary does, and the falsifiable core

The supporting queries that did run do not support H1.

The chalabhinna fixed collocation (query 07) peaks in LATE CANON, not in commentary. Per-Mchar densities: 1early 0.840, 2late 28.298, 3abh 0.264, 4para 9.070, 5comm 9.449, 6tika 4.126. The peak sits in 2late, and that 2late peak is single-work-driven: query 08 shows the great majority of fixed-collocation rows concentrate in one work (the Apadana), so this is a one-work concentration, not a distributed late-canon seam. Either way the commentary is not where the collocation is densest. Prediction H1(d) is falsified.

The abhinna NARROW-share (query 05) is non-monotonic, not a rising staircase: 1early 0.170, 2late 0.735, 3abh 0.167, 4para 0.591, 5comm 0.297, 6tika 0.210. There is no early-to-commentary climb, and the ambiguous fractions are large (508 of 1032 rows at 5comm, 350 of 701 at 6tika). Prediction H1(c) is falsified by the shape of the curve itself, independent of the coding gate discussed below.

The yamaka-patihariya counts (query 09) are small-N and concentrated in commentary (1early 0, 2late 6, 3abh 0, 4para 2, 5comm 126, 6tika 27). Per the small-N falsifier this is inconclusive for canon localization, not support either way.

The three-patihariya CRITIQUE structure (query 11) persists across canon and commentary rather than hardening in one place: per-Mchar 1early 0.458, 2late 0.260, 3abh 0.000, 5comm 0.489, 6tika 0.635. The canon's own self-critique of psychic display is present in both registers; it is not a commentary-era artifact.

The translator-divergence lane (query 13) shows English renderings lean overwhelmingly toward the open WIDE sense across translators: sujato 420 rows with 364 WIDE and 7 NARROW, thanissaro 87 with 28 and 3, nanamoli 44 with 5 and 0. This is a register fact about English glossing, consistent with abhinna not reading as the closed six-list to most translators.

### The sense-coding gate

The abhinna NARROW-vs-WIDE split (query 05) is gated on Cohen kappa from a frozen 172-row gold sample (query 06, md5-ordered on id). Three coders coded the sample blind. The deterministic pooled agreement computed downstream is far below the 0.6 threshold (Fleiss kappa about -0.14, mean pairwise Cohen about 0.06). That depressed figure is dominated by a label-vocabulary mismatch between coders: one coder used NARROW / WIDE / NEITHER, another used lowercase narrow / wide / ambiguous, and the pooled computation treats NEITHER and ambiguous as different categories. After normalizing label vocabulary (case-folded, NEITHER mapped to AMBIGUOUS), the two coder files available on disk agree on 119 of 172 three-way labels (Cohen kappa about 0.43), and on the binary NARROW-vs-rest split that query-05 actually rests on they agree on 165 of 172 rows (kappa about 0.83). The honest reading: coders agree well on the call that matters (is this row the fixed six-set or not) but disagree on WIDE-vs-AMBIGUOUS hairs, and one coder's differing vocabulary collapses the raw pooled kappa. Per the protocol falsifier, the recorded downstream kappa below 0.6 withdraws the query-05 NARROW-share as an auto-heuristic figure rather than an adjudicated one. This does not change the verdict: query-05 was already non-monotonic, so H1(c) fails whether or not the coding stands.

## The negative-control result

The negative control did NOT stay flat. The dukkha baseline (query 12) rose per-Mchar from 1early 79.950 to 5comm 132.057, a commentary-to-early ratio of 1.652, and to 6tika 93.696 (1.172). The robustness control paticca rose from 1early 20.923 to 5comm 28.379 (1.356) and to 6tika 32.760 (1.566), with a 3abh outlier of 248.918. The generic doctrinal baseline itself inflates into commentary. The program rule is explicit: a finding stands only if its negative control did not move with it. Here the control moved in the predicted direction. Any DISPLAY rise, had it been measured, would have needed to clear roughly the 1.65x dukkha band to be called supernatural-specific rather than generic commentary expansion. With the DISPLAY query missing, the only control comparison available shows the baseline already moving the way H1 predicts the signal would. The pre-committed H0 artifact arm gains. This is the mechanism that forces null rather than a deferred "no data."

## What it means and what it does not

It means: at the resolution of this corpus and these frozen lexica, no datable hardening seam in the supernatural-DISPLAY register is established, and the NARROW-vs-WIDE hardening of abhinna is not visible as a monotone early-to-commentary climb. The DIAGNOSTIC control is flat as predicted, but the DISPLAY axis it was meant to contrast against was never measured, and the negative control inflates into commentary on its own.

It does not mean: that the supernatural-display register does not harden in reality. The primary resolver timed out; this is a measurement gap, not a clean flat measurement. Re-running queries 01 and 03 with a raised statement timeout or a precomputed match column is the obvious next step before any stronger claim. It also does not mean any doctrinal verdict. This study makes no claim about whether psychic powers were taught, whether they are real, or whether commentary corrupted canon. The dating thesis (the fixed six-abhinna list as a later systematization) is prior published scholarship; this study's only original contribution was the per-character corpus-linguistic quantification of that known claim, and that contribution did not land.

## Auditability

Every count above re-derives from _protocol.json (the frozen lexica and enumeration SQL) and _raw.json (the query results), with no hand-entered numbers. The aggregate per-stratum rows for queries 02, 04, 05, 07, 09, 11, 12, 13 are read directly from _raw.json results. Queries 01 and 03 carry an explicit ERROR field "canceling statement due to statement timeout" and no rows. Derived ratios are arithmetic on those rows: dukkha 132.057/79.950 = 1.652; paticca 28.379/20.923 = 1.356; DIAGNOSTIC 5.572/5.803 = 0.960. The denominator cross-check is reported, not hidden: query 00_denominators_sanity returns live mula 26.9398 Mchar against the protocol's frozen 53.543921 (a ratio of 0.503), while attha and tika match frozen; the four canon strata (1early 13.0957 + 2late 3.8519 + 3abh 7.5768 + 4para 1.2128) sum to 25.737 Mchar, internally consistent with live mula. All per-Mchar magnitudes are relative to the run's own per-stratum denominators. The kappa figures re-derive from the coder files (_coding1_blind.json, _coding3.json) and the recorded _analysis.json iaa block.

## Scope and limits

Stratum is a work_slug lookup, never independent dating. The "seam" language describes corpus distribution, not historical events. Row-level match counting can conflate one dense passage with one sparse one. Distinct-works counts blur under SC-fine versus CST-coarse granularity. The NARROW-share operationalization is one reasonable proxy for PED's "nine in ten," not a proof of it, and it is currently withdrawn as auto-heuristic under the kappa gate. The frozen-denominator mismatch means all magnitudes are run-internal. The primary DISPLAY axis is unmeasured.
