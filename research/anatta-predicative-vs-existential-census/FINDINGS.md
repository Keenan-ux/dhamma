# Anattā predicative vs existential: a construction census of Wynne's distributional leg

## Abstract

This study asks a narrow corpus-linguistic question. Within the Pali corpus as held here, is the not-self teaching carried by the predicative construction (the threefold "n'etaṃ mama / n'eso'hamasmi / na meso attā" plus khandha-applied "X anattā"), with existential denial ("natthi attā") only marginal, and does the relative weight of generalizing and existential framing rise as one moves from canon to commentary to subcommentary. The test statistic is R, the ratio of existential-denial-plus-maxim density to predicative density, measured per million characters in each layer, with the prediction that R rises monotonically.

The result is the pre-committed null. R does not rise monotonically; per stratum it is non-monotone (1early 0.07, 2late 4.33, commentary 0.87, tika 0.74 in count form). Two negative controls both moved with the apparent signal. The reflexive attā control rises 20.1 to 302.7 to 205.5 per million characters across canon, commentary, and tika, far steeper than any R movement, which means R indexes generic attā-stem register and text length rather than the predicative-versus-existential construction. The anicca sister-maxim control also rises into commentary, steeper than the anattā maxim, so the maxim component of R is the generic "sabbe plus X plus mark" template proliferating in exegesis. There is no existential leg at any depth: strict "natthi attā" is negligible in every layer (canon 1 passage, tika 4, never above about 0.14 per million characters, an order of magnitude under the roughly 2 per million falsifier floor). What little R movement exists is the predicative denominator collapsing about thirteen times into commentary, not an existential numerator rising. Only the canon premise holds: predicative density beats existential denial by roughly 47 to 60 times in the early canon. The translator handle and the read-back diagnostic are null as flagged.

## The question and why it is a fair test

Wynne's distributional argument has a qualitative form already published in the secondary literature: early not-self language is predicative and the bare existential denial "there is no self" is marginal early, gaining relative weight only in later, more systematic framing. The novelty here is not that claim but a per-character layer census that can confirm or refute it as constructional density inside one held corpus.

The test is fair because it is falsifiable in both directions and the falsifiers were frozen before measurement. The frozen lexica separate five constructions plus two negative controls: the predicative triad, the khandha-applied bare predication, the universalizing maxim "sabbe dhammā anattā", existential denial "natthi attā", existential affirmation "atthi attā", the reflexive attā pronoun (attanā, attano, attānaṃ), and the anicca and dukkha sister-maxims. The reflexive control shares the attā stem but carries no not-self predication; if it tracks R, the effect is generic stem frequency, not construction. The sister-maxim control shares the syntactic mould but a different mark; if it tracks the anattā maxim, the effect is the maxim template, not anattā. The protocol stated in advance that the finding stands only if both controls stay flat.

Magnitudes are per character throughout, never raw rows. Layer is a work_role and work_slug lookup, not a dated chronology, so a layer difference is a register statement about the corpus as held, never independent corroboration of any relative-chronology claim.

## What the canon shows

The premise leg of Wynne's argument holds inside this corpus. In the early canon stratum the predicative triad runs at 4.58 per million characters and the khandha-applied bare predication at 2.06 per million characters, while existential denial "natthi attā" runs at 0.076 per million characters (one passage). The predicative construction therefore outweighs existential denial by roughly 47 to 60 times in the early canon, depending on whether the triad alone or the triad plus khandha predication is used as the numerator. This is consistent with the distributional leg as the text is held here. It is not independent corroboration of the historical argument, because layer is a lookup, not chronology.

The existential axis, where it appears at all, is debate register, not praxis teaching. Across the whole corpus existential affirmation outnumbers existential denial by 91 to 7. The co-occurrence and view-context queries show the existential pair sitting inside diṭṭhi and avyākata framing: the eternalist "sassato attā ca loko ca" formula, the Pañcattaya catalogue of self-after-death views, and the set-aside register where the question is declined. The 97-row export drawn for sense coding is dominated by exactly this material: 57 of 97 rows carry "sassato attā" and 53 of 97 carry a diṭṭhi term. Reading existential-denial density as a praxis teaching would misread quoted wrong-view and set-aside passages.

## What the commentary does, and the falsifiable core

The house thesis predicted a monotone rise of R across canon, commentary, and tika. It does not happen. In count form R is 0.07 at the earliest stratum, jumps to 4.33 at the late-canon stratum (driven entirely by a maxim spike on a tiny base, three predicative passages), and then sits at 0.87 in commentary and 0.74 in tika. That is non-monotone, and commentary above tika, the opposite of the predicted ordering. The single largest contributor to any apparent R movement downstream is the predicative denominator collapsing, from about 3.2 per million characters in the early canon to about 0.24 per million characters in commentary, roughly a thirteen-fold drop, because the supple predicative formula is not re-cited at high density in exegesis. The existential numerator never rises to compensate.

Two falsifiers fire here. Falsifier 4: existential denial is negligible at every depth, never crossing the roughly 2 per million character floor in any layer (canon 0.076, abhidhamma 0.000, commentary 0.000, tika 0.141 per million characters). There is no existential leg to rise; the rising-existential framing is not a corpus construction at any layer. Falsifier 2: the anicca sister-maxim control rises into commentary at 1.27 per million characters, steeper than the anattā maxim at 0.44 per million characters in the same layer, so the maxim component of R is the generic template proliferating in commentary, not anything specific to anattā.

The auditability arithmetic is also unresolved at the headline level. The analyst's reported R triple of 0.223 / 0.765 / 0.783 does not reconcile to the raw density query (which yields 0.143 / 1.733 / 1.478 per stratum, non-monotone, commentary above tika) nor cleanly to the raw count query. The protocol's stated million-character denominators for the canon-mula layer (53.54) do not match the dedup denominators in the run (the mula strata sum to about 26.9 million characters, while attha 29.49 and tika 28.36 do match). Any R triple must be recomputed and shown before publication; the directional claim is better stated in count form.

## The negative-control result

This is the decisive section. The reflexive attā control moved hard with the apparent signal. Its density rises 20.1 per million characters in the early canon to 302.7 in commentary to 205.5 in tika, a roughly ten to fifteen fold climb that dwarfs any R movement. The protocol rule is explicit: if the R-bearing layers also show elevated reflexive-attā density, the effect is generic attā-stem register and length, not the predicative-versus-existential construction, and the constructional reading is withdrawn. It is withdrawn. Falsifier 3 fires.

The sister-maxim control also moved, as noted above, firing falsifier 2. With both controls moving, the apparent R gradient cannot be attributed to anything specific about the predicative-versus-existential contrast. The honest reading is that commentary and subcommentary simply spend far more ink on the attā stem and on maxim templates generally, which is a statement about exegetical register, not about a shift in not-self construction.

The kappa gate is unrun. The 97-row existential sense audit (and the parallel anattā and reflexive audits) were exported for blind coding but the inter-annotator kappa has not yet been computed at the time of this writeup. Three blind coders have coded the existential-sense rows and agreement is high where their codings coincide, which supports the qualitative read that the existential bucket is quoted wrong-view and set-aside material rather than endorsed denial. That support extends only as far as the coders agree, and no anattā-derived or existential-derived density may be reported as trusted, with a contamination rate attached, until kappa clears 0.70.

## Secondary nulls

The translator handle is null as predicted. The form "no-self" never appears; "not-self" splits only by translator house style (sujato 105, thanissaro 22, with smaller counts elsewhere), and "non-self" appears a handful of times. This is translator habit, not a layer discriminator and not a doctrinal signal.

The commentary read-back diagnostic returned zero rows. The parallel graph carries no attha or tika links onto the early predicative anchors under the query as written, so the gloss-density diagnostic is unrun, not negative. It must be reported as a graph gap, never as evidence of low commentarial attention.

## What it means and what it does not

It means: inside this corpus, the canon premise of Wynne's distributional leg holds (predicative dominates existential denial by roughly 47 to 60 times early), but the predicted rising existential or generalizing gradient is absent, and the apparent gradient that exists is a measurement artifact of generic attā-stem register and the generic maxim template, both of which the frozen negative controls expose. The pre-committed null wins, with falsifiers 2, 3, and 4 all firing.

It does not mean Wynne is wrong about anything historical. This study cannot touch chronology (layer is a lookup), cannot touch the redactional leg, and cannot adjudicate whether early Buddhism taught no-self ontologically or strategically. A null here shows only that the predicative-versus-existential distinction is not differentially expressed as constructional density across the layers as held. It is not a doctrinal verdict.

## Scope limits

Corpus-linguistic only: density and register, never a doctrinal verdict. Layer is a work_role and work_slug lookup, not chronology; a layer difference is a register statement, not independent corroboration. Existential-denial densities rest on single-digit raw counts where one passage swings a per-million figure by about 0.08, so the per-character framing overstates precision and the small n is stated next to every density. No anattā-derived or reflexive-control density is trusted until the kappa 0.70 gate is run and the contamination rate reported. Foreign-language parallels are graph-only. Edition coarseness makes distinct-sutta counts edition-dependent. The null is a first-class result and does not refute Wynne; it shows his predicative-versus-existential distinction is not differentially expressed as density in this corpus.
