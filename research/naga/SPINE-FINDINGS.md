## Synthesis (the thesis, taking shape)
**"Faithful on the bare facts, innovative in the apparatus."** The canon already fixes (a) the four
births, (b) the animal-destination status, and (c) the soteriological ceiling — even *naming* it
(*aviruḷhidhamma*) and enacting it (the ordination bar) in Buddha-voice. The commentary's work is
*apparatus*: the Abhidhammic *akusala-vipāka-paṭisandhi* classification, the realm/diet detail
(water-dwelling, frog-eating), the 2→5 expansion of reversion-occasions, the broadening of
*tiracchāna*→*amanussa* (to Sakka), and above all the doctrinal **rationale** for the ceiling
(*abhabba* for jhāna/vipassanā/magga-phala). The nāga is the canon's exemplary *liminal* being: a moral
agent who keeps the sabbath and reveres the Buddha, yet is structurally barred from the path in this life
— the clearest single case of the canon's "human birth is the needed basis" principle.
