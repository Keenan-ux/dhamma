# Draft email to SuttaCentral

**Subject:** Dhamma Data — using your bilara-data and parallels graph

Dear SuttaCentral team,

A short thank-you and a heads-up. I've built a small scholarly tool
at <https://dhamma.fly.dev/> that leans heavily on your published
data: Bhante Sujato's nikāya translations as the primary English
layer (about 4,700 passage-aligned rows), Bhikkhu Brahmali's Vinaya
translations, the bilara-data Pāli text, John Kelly's Khuddaka
contributions, plus smaller sets from Bhante Suddhāso, Bhante
Kovilo, and Charles Patton. The sc-data/parallels.json file is
ingested in full (30,741 parallel, mention, and retells
relationships). In-corpus parallels render as clickable links in
the reader. Sanskrit, Chinese, and Gāndhārī parallels render as
plain references so the scholar can follow them on suttacentral.net.

The tool is non-commercial, ad-free, and telemetry-free. Each
SC-derived passage carries a per-card credit and a link back to
the corresponding suttacentral.net page. It sits next to the
ATI corpus and the recently-added BPS material (Visuddhimagga,
Bhikkhu Bodhi's commentary translations, Nyanaponika, Ñāṇamoli,
Ireland, Soma) as one of several scholarly translation layers
on top of the VRI/CST Pāli corpus and its commentaries. The
intent is to support comparative work across the Theravāda canon,
commentaries, sub-commentaries, and the surrounding ecosystem,
not to replace any authoritative source.

If you'd prefer different attribution wording, a different surface
treatment for the parallels graph, or have any concerns about the
way your data is being re-presented, please tell me and I'll adjust.

With deep gratitude for the open-data work SuttaCentral does.
