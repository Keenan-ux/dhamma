# Draft email to SuttaCentral

**Subject:** Dhamma Data — using your bilara-data and parallels graph

Dear SuttaCentral team,

A short thank-you and heads-up. I've built a small open-source
Buddhist studies tool at <https://dhamma.fly.dev/> that leans heavily
on your published data: Bhante Sujato's nikāya and Vinaya translations
as the primary translation layer, the bilara-data Pali text, and
sc-data/parallels.json — roughly 30,000 parallel / mention / retells
relationships, ingested in full. In-corpus parallels render as
clickable links in the reader; Sanskrit, Chinese, and Gāndhārī
parallels render as plain references so the scholar can follow them
on suttacentral.net.

The tool is non-commercial, ad-free, telemetry-free. Each Sujato
passage carries a per-card credit and links back to the corresponding
suttacentral.net page. Meant as a complement for comparative work
across the Theravāda canon, commentaries, sub-commentaries, and ATI's
library — not a replacement for the authoritative source.

If you'd prefer different attribution wording, a different surface
treatment for the parallels graph, or have any concerns about the way
your data is being re-presented, please tell me and I'll adjust.

With deep gratitude for the open-data work SuttaCentral does.

— [Your name]
