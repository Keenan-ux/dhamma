# Draft email to the Buddhist Publication Society

**To:** info@bps.lk, cnt@bps.lk
**Subject:** Permission request — Bhikkhu Bodhi's commentary translations for a non-commercial scholarly mirror

Dear BPS,

I'm writing on behalf of a small open-source scholarly tool I've
built at <https://dhamma.fly.dev/> for comparative Pali studies.
The site mirrors the Tipiṭaka with multi-translator coverage,
five integrated dictionaries, and the VRI/CST commentaries — no
ads, no tracking, no commercial features, non-commercial throughout.

Through Access to Insight's offline edition I already display, with
full attribution and a link back to your publications, 56 of
Bhikkhu Bodhi's BPS essays — the BPS Essays series, several Wheel
and Bodhi Leaves publications, and 13 of his sutta translations.
All are rendered under the CC BY-NC 4.0 terms ATI applied. You can
see an example at
<https://dhamma.fly.dev/library/ati-lib-authors-bodhi-bps-essay-09>
("A Look at the Kalama Sutta").

I'd like to ask whether you would be willing to extend the same
CC BY-NC 4.0 posture to four of Bhikkhu Bodhi's commentary
translation books — *The All-Embracing Net of Views* (BP209S),
*The Discourse on the Root of Existence* (BP210S), *The Discourse
on the Fruits of Recluseship* (BP211S), and *The Great Discourse on
Causation* (BP212S) — specifically so that the structured Aṭṭhakathā
translations they contain could be displayed alongside the
corresponding canonical passages on dhamma.fly.dev.

These four books would substantially lift the commentary
translation coverage available to scholars working in English.
Current coverage of the Pali Aṭṭhakathā with English translation
on the site is around 2.3%; even those four titles alone would
materially change what's queryable.

I want to be straightforward about why this is an ask rather than
a notification: BPS's general redistribution terms on the online
PDFs are for unaltered whole-file copies. Extracting commentary
passages into a structured database for cross-referencing is a
different posture than redistributing a PDF, and I'd rather have
explicit permission than presume. The CC BY-NC 4.0 precedent your
collaboration with ATI established makes a natural template.

If there's a different attribution form you'd prefer, or particular
material you'd rather I not include, please tell me and I'll
honour it. Happy to discuss by email or call.

With deep respect for the work BPS has done in making Pali Buddhist
scholarship accessible for over sixty years,

— [Your name]
