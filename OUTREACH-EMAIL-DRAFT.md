**To:** contact@buddhistinquiry.org
**Subject:** A mirror of the Access to Insight library at dhamma.fly.dev

Dear Barre Center for Buddhist Studies and the Access to Insight maintainers,

My name is [name]. I am writing to introduce myself and to say plainly what I have done with the
Access to Insight library, and to thank you for it.

I keep a small scholarly tool for reading and searching the Pali canon and its commentaries, at
https://dhamma.fly.dev. It is non-commercial. There is no advertising and no tracking. It exists so
that I and a few others can read these texts and their translations side by side.

I have downloaded the Access to Insight offline edition and preserved its library inside the tool,
under the CC BY-NC 4.0 terms it is offered on. Every translation and article carries its translator's
name and an attribution back to Access to Insight, with a link to your page. The non-canonical
material, the study guides, the author essays, the Thai forest collection, Path to Freedom, and the
glossary, sits in its own Library section rather than mixed in with the canonical texts. I took care
to keep each attribution intact. As one example, the Karaniya-metta Sutta at
https://dhamma.fly.dev/#/read/snp1.8 now carries five of your translations side by side, each named.

I know Access to Insight has been winding down, and I wanted you to hear directly from me that the
library is mirrored and being looked after, rather than to come across it on your own.

If anything about the attribution should read differently, or if there is material you would prefer I
take down, please tell me and I will make the change. I am happy to defer to your wishes on any of it.
And if it is ever useful, I would be glad to talk about hosting a stable mirror, so this material does
not have to depend on any one institution's budget to stay reachable.

With respect and gratitude for the work you have done,

[name]
[contact email]
